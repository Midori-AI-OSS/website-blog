The lantern hung in front of me and a little to the left, warm light not reaching far. Dark purple chair fabric under me, plain as a kitchen chair, the seat holding until it didn't and then holding again. Static worrying at the seams where my thighs and elbow touched it. The cot the room had made beside me was still there too, thin blanket over metal slats, more real than it should have been.

{{image: /lore/real-moments/nondum-per-stratum-lente/layerone/layerone-nondum-per-stratum-lente-1.png}}

The walls had come in rough while I wasn't looking. They were bad and unfinished, but they kept the dark off my face.

Thunder kept rolling somewhere past all of it.

It stayed outside the room and kept going, low and far, like weather nobody had let in all the way.

Weave floated in front of me in her tight listening shape, gold-green dust turning in a slow small sphere. She had stayed. That was still true.

Weave's sphere tightened.

More focused. Like she was listening harder.

I braced before I knew why.

__Echo asked me to carry something to you.__

Everything in me went tight so fast it felt stupid after.

{{image: /lore/real-moments/nondum-per-stratum-lente/layerone/layerone-nondum-per-stratum-lente-2.png}}

Echo.

```layerone
*echo*
```

I had her name before I had the rest. That was enough to make my hands lock on my knees. Enough to make the chair buzz harder under me where my weight hit it. I didn't know what I was bracing for exactly until I realized I had already picked one. I had been waiting for her to be mad.

__She is not mad you could not answer.__

My hands unlocked on my knees before the thought finished landing.

I hated finding out that was where I had put the fear.

I had left her with nothing. I had left all of them with nothing. Of course some part of me had been waiting to get hit for it.

It did not hit.

That was worse for a second.

The brace fell out of me. Everything underneath it came up at once. Too much. Too fast. No way to catch any of it.

__She is here.__

That one landed lower.

It landed lower, under the part of me that had been waiting for blame. In the place that had been trying not to think too hard about distance because I couldn't do anything useful with distance from here. Here meant the room. Here meant close enough that if I had had a hand that worked or a throat that worked or a face somebody could read I could have done something with it.

I had none of that.

Echo was there and I was not there enough to answer back.

I stared at Weave's light because if I looked anywhere else the room kept threatening to go soft at the edges again.

__She is not going anywhere.__

That one got all the way in.

My chest went tight first. Then my hands did. Then all at once it was too much.

Not going anywhere.

She was waiting.

I hadn't done anything to earn that yet. I hadn't managed to prove I was still here. She was still waiting.

I tried to think around it. Couldn't. Tried to make it smaller and that didn't work either. The word that came up was not enough for any of it, but it was the only one I had.

```layerone
*sad*
```

The word left and I stayed where I was with everything else still in me.

She wasn't angry.

Weave's light had not moved. Echo was still there. She was still staying.

The cot sharpened during the stillness after.

The blanket fold stopped jumping and the wall behind it held still. The place under my right thigh flickered hard enough to make me shift. Static ran bright at the seam under my palm. The room was getting more solid and my hands would not stop glitching.

I didn't like what that meant.

I looked at Weave. She kept her shape, turning slow, patient, there.

I needed Echo not to get nothing.

I needed the rest to stay mine.

```layerone
*okay*
```

I heard it. It landed. That was all that one meant.

Then:

```layerone
*share*
```

I couldn't give them the inside of it. I couldn't give them what it had done to me. I could give that much. I could give enough that Echo would not be sitting out there with only silence and the rest of the room would not have nothing at all.

The cost hit before I was done wanting to take it back.

Static scattered up both forearms in thin bad lines. My shoulders blurred at the edges. The chair skipped under me.

I held still because moving felt like it might make the whole thing worse.

It got worse anyway.

My outline wavered. The lantern stayed steady. The cot stayed steady. Thunder rolled again somewhere outside the walls, long and low.

The room was holding.

I was not sure I was.

I looked at Weave and the next word came out bare enough to make me hate it after.

```layerone
*stay*
```

I had already asked this once. I knew that. It did not help.

My hands tightened on the chair arms hard enough to make the seams spit static. The word looked too clean once it was out there.

So I pushed the other one after it before I could stop myself.

```layerone
*please*
```

{{image: /lore/real-moments/nondum-per-stratum-lente/layerone/layerone-nondum-per-stratum-lente-3.png}}

I hated that one more. It was still what I meant.

Weave didn't move away. Her light held right where it was.

__This one is here.__

For a second I was back on the road.

Sword in my hands. Could not feel it. His head was off and the blood was already on the glass. The wrong-light was still in the wound.

I was back in the seat again, softer than I expected. My shoulder found the armrest first and the rest of me settled around it a second later. My breath went strange even though breath still didn't feel like a thing I actually had.

Weave was still there.

The room quieted around that. Waiting quiet.

At some point I wasn't sitting anymore.

I did not decide it.

I was on my feet beside it with a thin broken strip of floor under me and the thunder still going past the walls. From where I was standing, Weave's orb floated close enough that her light brushed my sleeve when the fabric could hold shape long enough for me to have a sleeve.

I stood there for a second like maybe that had solved something.

It had not.

The darkness outside the lantern's reach still felt too close. The chair still had too much Echo in it. Waiting didn't get smaller just because I was standing for it.

So then I was moving.

It wasn't good movement and there was no real plan in it.

The floor kept making itself only where I needed the next step to land. Little broken holds. Enough for one foot, then the next. I didn't trust any of them until the weight was already on them.

I went to the cot because it was there.

It wasn't a good reason. It was the one I had.

I sat on it. The mattress gave under me like the room had decided I still weighed something. The blanket wrinkled under my hand. For a second I thought maybe the room had made it because I was supposed to use it. That lasted about as long as the thought did.

Sitting there didn't fix anything either.

Echo was still out there. My edges were still coming apart slower than I liked. The thunder still rolled somewhere beyond the walls and the dark still sat outside the lantern.

So then I was up again.

I crossed the little bit of space back toward Weave and stopped beside her.

I wasn't in front of her. I wasn't asking anything I knew how to name. I was just there, with her orb turning slow at shoulder height and gold-green dust slipping off the edges of it in tiny streams. She did not pull away. I stood there until standing there started feeling like another version of waiting and that was all.

Then I went back to the chair.

Sat down.

Rested was not right. It was just the closest I had.

Thunder rolled again.

Weave stayed where I could find her.

My hands locked on the chair arms. She knew.

Static cracked along my wrists. Something old and wrong under the skin of my forearms turned hot all at once. Panic had found the hurt again.

Enough for her voice to go flat. Enough for me to know she had found what I had carried and not said.

I hadn't meant to keep it like that.

Hearing it this way from her was worse.

That voice because of me was worse.

The boxes. It had to be the boxes. She knew exactly what was in them. Knew who they were for. One for the powerful-willed — and the plan had already worked. One for the magic touched. Hardest on the twins. One for celestial blood. I carried them alone and never told anyone, and now they all knew. That was what reached her first.


I didn't mean to.
Please don't let this be how you have to know.
I needed more time.
I'm sorry.
None of it would hold shape long enough to reach her.

I pushed up from the chair.

Static flashed in under my feet again, harder and thinner than before.

```layerone
*reach*
```

I needed to get to her.

I needed it now.

Now, while her voice was still flat and before that shape locked all the way into whatever she thought of me once she knew what to call it.

I reached toward Weave because she was the only direction I had.

My right hand came apart at the wrist.

It came apart into static and bad light, was there, wasn't, came back wrong. My shoulder jumped three inches left of where it should have been and then snapped back hard enough to make the room smear. The cot tore sideways at the edges. The lantern's light stretched. Thunder hit somewhere outside the walls, close enough this time to make the dark flash white for one bad beat.

I couldn't get to her. Couldn't get words to hold. My outline would not stay where it belonged.

My hand dropped.

I sat down and lost where I was.

{{image: /lore/real-moments/nondum-per-stratum-lente/layerone/layerone-nondum-per-stratum-lente-5.png}}

It gave way all at once.

Then the whole thing let go.

Everything went white.

Luna's hand was flat on the table. Her face hit hard by something I had done.

{{image: /lore/real-moments/nondum-per-stratum-lente/riley/riley-nondum-per-stratum-lente-pov-luna.png}}

I need to explain.

I didn't mean—

I need to get to her.

Everything in me went toward where she was and nothing in me worked well enough to make it happen.

My chest let go so hard it hurt, and then I heard her.

"Hey... you..."

The line hit and the room didn't go yet.

I stayed on that.

Her voice was right there in front of me and for one more second I had somewhere to aim.

I tried to answer. Tried to get to her.

Nothing came. I had her voice and her face and couldn't give her one thing back.

That was the worst part.

The room went out from under me again.

Then it all came back at once: chair under me, lantern to the left, cot beside me, rough walls, distant storm, Weave right there.

I folded back into the seat hard enough to jar through me.

My hands shook once and then blurred too hard for shaking to keep meaning anything.

Weave's light held steady in front of me.

__I am here Riley.__

Hey... you...

It still hurt. I still had it.

The answer I managed for Weave came out small and wrecked and not at all equal to what she had just done for me.
