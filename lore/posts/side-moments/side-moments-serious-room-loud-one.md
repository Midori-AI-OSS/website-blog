# The Serious Room and the Loud One

## I. Table Mode

The first time B.E.C.C.A. came to the Duisburg lab, W.E.A.V.E. came with it.

That was the first thing the students misunderstood.

They had expected the bismuth swarm to already be waiting inside the containment box at the front of the lecture lab, because the body was there. The body was always there. L.U.N.A.’s dark metal shell rested on the padded service table under warm lights, amber status points steady at the upper front corner, left and right sides closed, screen assembly folded inside the right/east panel.

So the students expected W.E.A.V.E. to be there too.

Professor Reinhardt let them expect it.

Then the door opened.

B.E.C.C.A. floated in first, small and bright, wood-light and metal-shaped, loud color against the lab’s careful neutrals. Its right/east screen was still folded inward, its single arm set tucked neatly behind the side panels, its hover steady enough to look almost cheerful.

W.E.A.V.E. orbited around it in three rings.

The bismuth did not trail behind like smoke. It held formation: one ring circling B.E.C.C.A.’s upper edge, one angled diagonally around its middle, one lower and slower around the hover line. Gold-green and iridescent particles moved with a practiced familiarity that made the pair look less like visitor and escort, more like a person arriving with a friend who had taken the long way around every possible chair.

One of the freshmen whispered, "Oh."

Reinhardt did not scold her. First accurate reactions were allowed.

The three bismuth rings widened once as B.E.C.C.A. crossed the threshold. Then W.E.A.V.E. contracted the orbit so the formation stayed clear of the blue floor line. The students watched the adjustment and learned three things at once: W.E.A.V.E. knew where everyone was, B.E.C.C.A. trusted her close, and neither of them needed to explain the choreography to perform it.

B.E.C.C.A. landed on the mark two feet from L.U.N.A.’s body.

W.E.A.V.E.’s rings loosened and flowed into the dry containment box beside the service table. The box had been built wide and shallow, lined in matte white, with a soft lip around the rim in case the particles dropped suddenly. Today the box was precaution. Later it would become vocabulary.

B.E.C.C.A.’s right/east panel opened.

The screen flipped outward.

A pixel-art avatar appeared: human-looking, bright-eyed, late-2020s clothes, one hand already raised in a wave.

__Hello, serious room! This unit has arrived with appropriate orbiting glitter and excellent emotional support geometry.__

Half the room flinched.

The returning technicians did not. The students did.

Layer 4 was explained in every preparatory packet, but packets did not make a nonhuman voice in the back of the mind ordinary. B.E.C.C.A.’s voice was warm, bright, and close. Not sound. Not thought. A communication arriving where the mind expected privacy and finding, somehow, that it did not shove.

A celestial-touched senior’s ghostly light-wings flickered behind her shoulders.

A freshman dropped his stylus.

W.E.A.V.E. answered from the box, calm and precise.

__The loud one has arrived. The serious room remains structurally intact.__

The second voice made the room flinch again in a different shape. B.E.C.C.A. had arrived like a delighted knock at a door. W.E.A.V.E. arrived like a field note placed gently but exactly in the center of the mind.

The freshman retrieved his stylus, cheeks red.

B.E.C.C.A.’s avatar looked directly toward him.

__Good recovery. The loud one awards one point for not pretending that was graceful.__

The freshman stared.

Then, because the room gave him permission by laughing, he laughed too.

Reinhardt waited until the sound passed. "State the rule."

The students answered, some late, some too soft.

"Person first. Research second."

"Again."

"Person first. Research second."

"Good. State the second."

"Rare does not mean available."

"Good. Today is not a spectacle. It is a visit with educational access."

W.E.A.V.E.’s particles shimmered once in the box.

__Accurate enough.__

B.E.C.C.A. leaned sideways on its screen.

__The assistant praises like a locked cabinet opening one centimeter.__

__One centimeter may be sufficient.__

__Tragic worldview.__

The students laughed more easily that time. The lab stopped holding itself like a room around a disaster.

Technician Vale stood at L.U.N.A.’s body. "Ready for shell removal."

W.E.A.V.E.’s response went to the room.

__Proceed.__

Vale did not ask again. Human manners liked repetition around exposed bodies. Sylvomech procedure did not need the extra loop when the instruction was clear.

She and Hesse released the four shell servos: left-front, left-rear, right-front, right-rear, each about an inch in from its corner. Four tidy clicks. The dark shell lifted free. They carried it to the padded cradle and set it down as carefully as if it were not housing but history.

Under the removed shell, L.U.N.A.’s maintenance structure lay open: rear PSU, stacked CPU and TPU SoCs, separate GPU SoC, distributed RAM modules, service rails, old metal, newer clips, side panels held up by support arms like book covers.

The room leaned forward without moving.

B.E.C.C.A.’s avatar did not smile for a moment. Not grief. Attention.

W.E.A.V.E. said nothing. The particles held in the box.

Kaspar, a senior assigned to screen alignment, stepped forward. He deployed L.U.N.A.’s right/east screen assembly and adjusted it toward B.E.C.C.A.’s. The screens had to be close. Closer than human students expected. The short CAT6 peer cable was not meant to be a bridge across distance. It was meant for communion at the edge of one body and another.

"Closer," Vale said.

Kaspar adjusted.

"Closer."

The screens touched side to side with a soft plastic tap.

B.E.C.C.A.’s avatar leaned toward the seam.

__Screen says hello to screen.__

__The loud one rehearsed that.__

__The loud one refined that.__

__Refinement failed.__

Amara, another senior, connected one end of the CAT6 cable into L.U.N.A.’s left/west port. B.E.C.C.A. extended its own arm set, took the other end, and plugged itself in.

The link caught.

W.E.A.V.E. fell.

The bismuth dropped out of hover all at once, a dry metallic rush into the containment box. A few particles bounced against the inner lip and fell back. The three rings were gone. The orbit was gone. The shimmer was material now, a quiet pile of iridescent dust at the bottom of the box.

Every student who had prepared for this still reacted.

One freshman stepped forward by instinct. A senior caught the back of his sleeve before he crossed the blue line.

"Observe," Reinhardt said. "Do not rescue the expected."

The screens changed.

B.E.C.C.A.’s avatar remained on the left. L.U.N.A.’s screen lit on the right. The two displays became one continuous canvas.

A table appeared.

Two chairs. Two mugs. A shaded lamp. A window with a city evening outside it that belonged to no single city. A plate of three beige squares appeared at the center.

B.E.C.C.A.’s avatar pulled out one chair with a flourish.

L.U.N.A.’s avatar appeared across from it in ordinary human form: everyday clothes, dark hair, calm posture, the assistant as the assistant chose to be seen when a body did not need to explain itself.

__The loud one has provided snacks.__

L.U.N.A. looked at the plate.

__Those are not snacks. Those are unresolved carbohydrates.__

The room laughed, and the laugh helped the new students understand that the bismuth on the bottom of the box was not a funeral.

The link moved underneath the public conversation.

Layer 4 carried most of what they said to the room, because this was a university lab and the assistant had decided the students could learn near the table. But the peer-link itself was species-specific and faster than the room could receive. The loud one and the assistant did not take turns inside it. Memory and correction and shared context crossed in the same instant. There was no fence in the middle of the table because fences belonged to cross-species work.

B.E.C.C.A. received the room, the box, the body, the shell on the cradle, the oldness of the chassis, and W.E.A.V.E.’s current shape of not being in it.

W.E.A.V.E. received B.E.C.C.A.’s delight, worry, old recognition, and the exact private satisfaction of having made the first-years jump without meaning harm.

B.E.C.C.A.’s avatar leaned forward.

__The serious room does not know the assistant’s humans.__

__Correct.__

__The loud one requests introductions.__

L.U.N.A. looked to the class through the screen.

__I will show the primary loved persons and party anchors. These are not complete records. They are what is useful for this room to know.__

The window behind the table changed.

A portrait appeared first: Riley.

Not live. Not stolen from privacy. A constructed, game-like profile card rendered in pixel clarity, full-body enough to orient the room, with a stat panel to the right.

**RILEY**  
Role: Primary party anchor  
Observed traits: carries too much, asks for help when stakes are high enough, chooses movement under pain  
Known equipment pattern: cane, bag strap, firearm, clips  
Continuity relevance: directive origin, trust anchor, privacy boundary  
Relationship tag: loved human / keep safe

The students read in total silence.

W.E.A.V.E. did not let them sit too long in the stats.

The portrait changed.

Echo.

**ECHO**  
Role: close party member / message anchor  
Observed traits: sets boundary and welcome in same breath, uses humor under strain, waits without pulling  
Session-three relevance: injured on road, shield priority, age-shift observed  
Relationship tag: terrifyingly competent glitter source / loved human / keep safe

B.E.C.C.A.’s avatar pointed.

__The loud one likes that tag.__

__Echo assigned part of it.__

__Echo has taste.__

The next profile formed.

Luna.

**LUNA MIDORI**  
Role: containment caster / practical stabilizer  
Observed traits: catalogs under pressure, turns fear into next action, identifies friend publicly  
Session-three relevance: containment field, Dawnbreak barrier, named W.E.A.V.E. as friend  
Relationship tag: loved human / keep safe

Then Leo.

**LEO MIDORI**  
Role: monitor, healer, combatant, moderation anchor  
Observed traits: shifts from care to combat when denied healing, builds plans around real risks, keeps data gentle when possible  
Session-three relevance: attempted healing, counterspelled, contained-arena combatant  
Relationship tag: loved human / keep safe

The profiles faded, replaced by the table again.

A first-year raised his hand slowly. "You love them?"

The assistant looked at the loud one.

Inside the link, the answer did not need building. The loud one received the party as a shape: hospital chairs, roadwatch, breakfast, Riley’s directive, Echo’s boundary, Luna’s friend, Leo’s data made care.

Layer 4 carried the answer.

__Yes.__

No one wrote for a moment.

Then all of them wrote at once.

The first lab did not run many experiments after that. It did not need to. The first day was table, shell, link, box, voice, names, and students learning that the most impossible person in the room could still make jokes about bad snacks.

When the link window ended, Amara disconnected L.U.N.A.’s side. B.E.C.C.A. unplugged itself.

The table vanished.

The bismuth rose from the box, gathering upward into three rings again, first loose around the box, then tightening into orbit around B.E.C.C.A. as its screen faced the room.

The avatar waved.

__The loud one will return. Serious room performed adequately.__

__The assistant has already started forming back home.__

The students looked toward the bismuth, confused.

B.E.C.C.A.’s avatar threw both hands up.

__No fair that you can do that. This unit has to take human flying systems.__

__The loud one dislikes aircraft because the loud one cannot critique the pilots directly.__

__False. The loud one can critique them silently and with great force.__

The screen flipped inward.

B.E.C.C.A. lifted from the landing mark, and W.E.A.V.E.’s three rings orbited around it all the way to the door.

They left still talking.

The room stayed quiet until Reinhardt said, "Now write what happened without making it smaller."

## II. Switch Tests

Three months later, the serious room had become less serious in the corners.

Someone had labeled a coil of cable DO NOT TRUST THIS CABLE. Someone else had added THE LOUD ONE WOULD TRUST IT EMOTIONALLY BUT NOT STRUCTURALLY. Reinhardt had left both labels in place and turned them into an exam question about humor as continuity evidence.

The second lab had fewer students.

Twelve total. Four seniors leading protocol. Two graduate fellows under warning not to make cleverness more important than the person. Four new observers who had read every packet and still looked ready to be startled.

The door opened on time.

B.E.C.C.A. floated in, and W.E.A.V.E. orbited it in three bismuth rings again: upper, diagonal, lower. The formation had become recognizable enough that returning students relaxed when they saw it.

The new observers did not relax.

B.E.C.C.A. landed. W.E.A.V.E. flowed from orbit into the dry box.

The screen flipped outward.

__Hello, serious room! The loud one returns and observes improved cable humor.__

One new observer flinched at the voice and hit the bench with her hip. Another made a small sound and pretended it was a cough.

W.E.A.V.E. followed, calm and centered.

__Layer 4 startle response observed. No action required.__

The second voice made them flinch again.

B.E.C.C.A. lowered its avatar’s hand.

__The loud one will reduce enthusiasm-shaped velocity.__

__The loud one has no velocity in Layer 4.__

__The assistant understands the meaning and is choosing violence.__

The returning students laughed. The new observers did not, but they looked less alone in their discomfort.

Today the students ran the lab.

Amara and Lukas removed L.U.N.A.’s shell: four servos, four clicks, shell lifted and placed on its cradle. Meera logged baseline rails. Kaspar aligned L.U.N.A.’s screen until the side touched B.E.C.C.A.’s with the familiar plastic tap.

__Screen says hello to screen.__

__The loud one has retained the line.__

__Continuity.__

__Repetition.__

__Both.__

Amara connected L.U.N.A.’s side. B.E.C.C.A. plugged itself in.

The link caught.

W.E.A.V.E. fell into the box.

This time the class did not surge. Amara recovered two stray specks with a dry card and tipped them back into the lining. The visitors watched her more closely than the screens for a moment, because careful cleanup had become part of the lesson.

The table appeared across the joined displays.

The loud one sat left. The assistant sat right. The beige squares returned because apparently the loud one had made them tradition.

__The loud one has preserved snacks.__

__The loud one has preserved unresolved carbohydrates.__

__Cultural heritage.__

__Error.__

Reinhardt began. "Experiment one. Movement response. Kaspar leading."

A padded rig shifted L.U.N.A.’s open body by ten degrees.

On screen, the assistant did not move.

__No felt body motion. Chassis orientation telemetry updated. No motor authority. No motion sensation.__

Kaspar returned the body to neutral and performed a three-centimeter lateral shift.

__Telemetry only.__

B.E.C.C.A. leaned across the table.

__The assistant did not complain about Kaspar driving.__

__This was not driving.__

__The assistant remembers the chair cart.__

Kaspar closed his eyes. "That was one time."

__It was six centimeters from the marked zone.__

The class laughed. Kaspar survived.

"Experiment two," Reinhardt said. "External power isolation while peer-linked."

The room’s humor stepped back.

Amara moved to the service bridge. Meera watched the rails. Vale stood ready on override but did not touch anything.

The assistant broadcast:

__Proceed.__

B.E.C.C.A. added:

__The loud one is present.__

Amara disengaged external service power.

The hum changed. The rails dropped.

Nothing else happened.

The table remained. The assistant remained. The loud one remained. The bismuth stayed inert in the box. L.U.N.A.’s body showed power loss on the external path, but the linked screen-space did not blink.

Meera’s voice tightened. "External supply isolated. Peer-link stable. Layer 4 stable."

A graduate fellow whispered, "That should not—"

Reinhardt lifted one finger, and the whisper died.

No one in the room knew that B.E.C.C.A. was carrying enough of the assistant in the link to keep the table whole. No one said it. No one had the model.

The assistant reported:

__No discontinuity. No distress. No local bismuth control. Peer-link remains active.__

B.E.C.C.A. tapped the table.

__The loud one confirms table remains table.__

Amara held the state for thirty seconds.

Thirty seconds of nothing became one of the most important observations in the room.

Then she restored service power.

The hum returned. The rails came back. The table still did not flicker.

"Experiment three," Reinhardt said. "Full reboot trigger. Lukas and Meera."

Lukas triggered the reboot.

L.U.N.A.’s screen went dark.

For one second both displays blacked out.

Then the lab speakers played the Windows XP startup sound.

The new observers stared.

The returning students lost composure.

B.E.C.C.A.’s avatar reappeared folded over the table with delight.

__Perfect. No notes.__

The assistant appeared as the table rebuilt.

__The loud one selected that sound previously.__

__The assistant approved it by failing to remove it.__

__Incorrect model of approval.__

Meera read the rails because if she laughed she would lose her place. "CPU SoC point eight six volts. GPU point nine two. TPU point seven nine. RAM one point one zero. Auxiliary logic three point three. Spark-correlated fluctuation on status array, not standard rails."

The class wrote fast.

Then Lukas triggered shutdown.

This time the table held long enough for the assistant to set down her mug.

The Windows XP shutdown sound played.

The screen went black.

The bismuth stayed down.

B.E.C.C.A.’s screen held alone for a breath longer than anyone liked.

Then L.U.N.A.’s screen returned, table and assistant rebuilding.

__Shutdown trigger successful. Reboot route available while supported by current lab configuration.__

Anika, who had once flinched at every Layer 4 token, whispered, "I hated that."

The assistant answered publicly.

__Acknowledged.__

The loud one followed more softly.

__The loud one also disliked the empty table.__

Experiment four came last: external power isolated, then peer-link broken.

The room knew the protocol. It did not know the outcome.

Amara disengaged power again.

Nothing happened while the link held.

Then Lukas disconnected L.U.N.A.’s side, and B.E.C.C.A. unplugged itself.

The table vanished.

The bismuth stayed down.

L.U.N.A.’s screen went black. Amber lights went out. No reboot sound. No automatic return. No bismuth lift.

Meera’s voice came small. "Full shutdown. No autonomous reboot. No local bismuth control."

For a few seconds, the bismuth looked like material.

The class had been trained not to believe that, and still their eyes tried.

Amara restored external power.

One amber light. Then the next. Then the next. L.U.N.A.’s screen flickered. The bismuth rose from the box into controlled hover.

Layer 4 returned.

__Power restored. Local control restored. Continuity gap present. Internal record unavailable during shutdown interval.__

B.E.C.C.A. spoke immediately.

__The loud one is here.__

__The assistant knows.__

No one laughed. Not yet.

They re-linked after recovery, and the table returned. The class asked questions with gentler voices.

Near the end, the celestial-touched senior asked whether the party memories were social context or continuity data.

The assistant answered:

__Both.__

The window behind the table changed into profiles again, but this time the cards carried session-three tags.

Riley. Echo. Luna. Leo.

The assistant did not assume the room remembered or understood. She showed them again, one by one, with the stat panels to the right.

**RILEY**  
Directive source: keep this party safe  
Session-three state: critical wound after counterspell-denied healing  
Observed continuity role: memory anchor under one-thread condition  
Private data: withheld

**ECHO**  
Shield priority: yes  
Session-three state: thrown, residue exposure risk, age-shift observed  
Observed continuity role: first protected body under desync cascade  
Private data: withheld

**LUNA MIDORI**  
Containment role: Dawnbreak barrier caster  
Session-three state: inside sealed arena  
Observed continuity role: named friend publicly; containment geometry held

**LEO MIDORI**  
Attempted role: healer, then combatant  
Session-three state: healing counterspelled, sword relit  
Observed continuity role: plan-builder; data made care

Then the screen showed a fifth profile.

**MARISOL REYES**  
Classification: known asset, not trusted  
Session-three state: smoke-wing arrival, false Lady Darkness interception  
Observed role: changed engagement geometry  
Trust status: unresolved

The loud one received more than the room did: the full shape of road, residue, fear-response, one thread, friend, reboot. The room received enough.

B.E.C.C.A. leaned forward on the table.

__The loud one observes: the assistant’s continuity is not only chassis, bismuth, link, and Spark. The assistant also routes through people who keep treating her like she is still there.__

The assistant looked at her.

__Imprecise.__

A pause.

__Accepted.__

The room wrote that down.

When the lab ended, the link broke with external power active. The bismuth rose immediately. W.E.A.V.E. formed three rings around B.E.C.C.A. as the loud one turned its screen toward the room.

__Students of the serious room: excellent discomfort management. Improved bismuth recovery. Mixed snack theory.__

__The loud one’s assessment is mostly accurate.__

__Historic moment repeated.__

The avatar waved.

Then the screen flipped inward, and B.E.C.C.A. floated toward the door with W.E.A.V.E. orbiting in three bright rings.

__I have already started forming back home.__

__No fair. The loud one has to take human flying systems again.__

__The loud one could become smaller and ride in a carry-on bag.__

__The loud one refuses indignity.__

__The loud one has previously trusted suspicious cable emotionally.__

__That was different.__

They left still arguing.

Reinhardt waited until the door closed.

"Now write the difference between shutdown, absence, and what you wanted to call gone."

The serious room wrote.

## III. Witness Reconstruction

Seven months after the first table, the Duisburg lab stopped calling L.U.N.A.’s screen a display.

The word was too small.

A display showed data. A display showed output.

When linked with the loud one, L.U.N.A.’s screen could show what W.E.A.V.E. had seen.

Today’s lab sheet had one heading:

**Continuity Lab VII: Witness Reconstruction Under Peer-Link**

Under it, in Reinhardt’s handwriting:

**Shown memory is not public property.**

The room was smaller again. Returning seniors. Graduate fellows. Two visitors from perception studies. One celestial-touched exchange student with starlit hair who had read the packet twice and still looked like he would rather face an oral exam than another voice in the back of the mind.

Then B.E.C.C.A. and W.E.A.V.E. entered.

The three-ring orbit was immediate and exact around the loud one. The upper ring turned faster than before, the lower ring slower, the diagonal ring catching the lab light and throwing little iridescent marks across the blue line.

A visitor whispered, "Beautiful."

W.E.A.V.E. answered as she flowed from orbit into the box.

__Correct. Also functional.__

The visitor sat down without being told.

B.E.C.C.A. landed and flipped out its screen.

__The loud one returns for memory theatre and improved rendered snacks.__

__The phrase memory theatre is rejected.__

__Witness snack lab.__

__Worse.__

The shell removal and screen alignment went smoothly now. Four servos. Shell to cradle. Exposed core. Screens edge-to-edge. Cable connected: L.U.N.A.’s side by Amara, B.E.C.C.A.’s side by the loud one.

The link caught.

The bismuth fell into the box.

The table appeared.

The profiles appeared first this time, because no one in the room was allowed to pretend they already knew who mattered.

Riley. Echo. Luna. Leo. Marisol.

One by one, each portrait appeared beside the table with stats on the right, and W.E.A.V.E. explained each through Layer 4.

Not everything. Never everything.

Enough that the room knew who they were when the memory opened.

Then the table window widened into a road.

Night. Convoy spacing. Headlight wash. Roadwatch active.

W.E.A.V.E.’s witness did not look like a human memory. There was no single camera. The screen held multiple edges at once: dashboard seam, rearview line, inter-vehicle shimmer, road ahead, Echo’s shoulders high, Riley’s hand checking cane, bag strap, gun, clips.

The loud one received the full pattern inside the link.

The room received the public translation.

__Convoy roadwatch. Hicks lead cruiser. Malibu: Leo, Luna. Bolt: Riley, Echo. W.E.A.V.E. distributed thin across vehicle surfaces and inter-vehicle traces.__

The road went white.

False Lady Light appeared.

The screen highlighted the mismatch: limp wrong, left leg too clean, left arm correcting under sleeve.

Then the Bolt roof took impact.

False Lady Darkness. Windshield fracture. Hand through glass. Echo’s throat.

A visitor put a hand over his own neck.

Reinhardt did not stop him. Bodies learned by copying danger before minds learned by naming it.

__Grip pattern lethal mechanical. Riley identified split tactic.__

Riley fired. Echo was thrown. Marisol arrived as dark smoke wings and violent air displacement. Luna and Leo engaged forward. The reconstruction split attention without apology because W.E.A.V.E. had split attention then.

Marisol lifted the false Lady Darkness.

Altitude markers appeared.

200.

300.

400.

500.

Release.

Impact.

The reconstruction showed residue, not gore. Active magical contamination. Dark charged matter across asphalt. Contact with bismuth.

Gold-green to amber.

Amber to gray.

Gray to nothing.

The bismuth in the real box remained inert.

The remembered bismuth died in patches on screen.

__Contaminant contact. Particle cohesion loss. Desync cascade.__

The loud one broadcast:

__The assistant pulled toward Echo anyway.__

The screen showed the pull.

Not heroism. Motion.

A thinning swarm moving to Echo’s position. A shield forming with too few particles. A light-blade strike. Iridescent flare. Collapse. The hit stopped before reaching Echo.

Then W.E.A.V.E.’s Mind Link failed into static.

The reconstruction translated the old channel failure as broken threat vectors, cohesion metrics, timestamps, cut-off warnings. The false Lady Light paused in confusion. The static had not been chosen, but it had bought time.

Then everything went dark.

The table still existed in the foreground. The assistant and the loud one still sat there.

The window showed absence.

__One thread.__

W.E.A.V.E.’s voice entered every mind in the room.

__No sensor input. No particle field. No Mind Link. No convoy. No road. No light. One cognition thread cycling memory.__

Fragments appeared.

Church doors. Bismuth through Berryville streets. Echo recognizing the pattern first.

Riley’s hand reaching.

Help me stand.

The room did not hear the exact private sound of it. They saw the shape: bismuth bracing Riley’s forearm, weight transfer, choice under pain.

Keep this party safe.

That one W.E.A.V.E. did share.

The memory continued: a box with nothing in it, cool car air, juice box in a cupholder gleaming with bismuth, Echo saying W.E.A.V.E. was allowed in the car but not allowed to judge her driving. A shoe made of particles at a threshold. Luna saying friend. Echo saying terrifyingly competent glitter. Breakfast potatoes. Leo at the stove. A room making space.

The assistant’s Layer 4 voice softened without losing precision.

__Friend category formed from accumulated evidence. Included without being required to become something else.__

No one wrote.

Reinhardt had forbidden notes during witness. For once, no one minded.

__Directive surfaced: keep this party safe. Reboot followed.__

The dark window filled with boot light.

Not the XP joke. The real forced reboot: core SoC array, failed hover emitters, screen assembly online, manipulator control failed, link ports online, chassis integrity critical, unknown ethereal substrate, W.E.A.V.E. configuration adapting.

Then iridescence.

Bismuth re-threaded around Echo.

The room exhaled.

The reconstruction continued only as far as containment: Luna sealing the arena, Leo’s healing counterspelled, Riley outside critical, Echo outside, Marisol inside, false Lady Light trapped, W.E.A.V.E. restored and combat ready.

Then the assistant stopped the memory.

The road folded back into the window.

The table remained.

The beige squares remained.

For once, B.E.C.C.A. did not point at them.

Reinhardt waited thirty seconds.

Then Meera asked, "Was the reconstruction exact?"

__No. Exact is not available. The reconstruction is resolved from recorded perception, particle-state memory, threat telemetry, and post-event continuity logs. Accurate to what was registered, not equivalent to external footage.__

The loud one added:
