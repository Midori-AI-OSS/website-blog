The night is still full of movement.

Two figures approaching from the cop-car lineup. I see them in pieces — the way you see things when your head will not track and your eyes do not focus right. One of them has hair that catches the headlights. Pale. Almost white in the glare. The other stays in her shadow. Smaller. Quieter.

Lady Light. Lady Darkness.

They are moving carefully. Limping. The kind of careful that comes from somewhere deep. I am too far to see why.

They stop in front of Luna and Leo. Shapes. Posture. An exchange I cannot hear.

Then her voice is in the back of my head. Steady. Precise. The words arrive whole — the way they do when she is reporting something, not checking in.

__Riley deployed the Lacuna Crystalli inside the sealed box. The caster's sequence trust fractured — his spells failed, backfired. She fought him hand-to-hand. The Fracture blade made contact — shoulder, ribs, then the neck. Decapitation. The crystal was returned to the safety case and sealed. She collapsed immediately after. The through-and-through wound from the earlier clone encounter was sealed by Luna. Riley is stable but unconscious. Exhaustion, not injury.__

Riley's name. In her voice. I lift my head. A small motion. Just enough. My fingers stop moving in her hair for a beat. The words keep coming — the box, the blade, the neck, the collapse. Things I watched through the glass but could not process. Things I am still not processing.

Then my name.

__Echo: cervical compression injury from the false Lady Darkness clone — limited vocal output. Blunt cranial impact against the driver-side window. Residue exposure from the destroyed clone — visible age progression. Approximately five years.__

I lift my head again. Sharper this time. My own body — the throat, the head, the residue, the years — listed out in her steady, precise voice. Like a report. The words land strange. Wrong in a way I cannot name. Not wrong because they are false. Wrong because I am sitting here holding Riley with hands that are not my hands and she is telling the whole dark road what the years meant.

Five years.

She is still talking. Weave. The words keep coming and I am too slow to stop them.

Cause. The residue. Exposure from the clone's destruction. She names it like a cause of death and I suppose it is close enough. The thing that got in my mouth. My throat. The dust on my skin that is still drying. That clone. The one Riley broke open through the glass. It gave me five years. Left its dust in me like a signature. I do not know if that is a gift or just a fact but my face is wrong and my hands are wrong and the number is five.

Another name. Not mine. Leo.

Right wrist. Multiple fracture. Initial engagement.

I cannot turn my head. My neck will not do it. I cannot look through the window to see where he is. If he is still walking. If he is in the cruiser already. If the arm he was cradling is the one. I knew he was hurt. I saw the careful way he moved across the road. But I did not know it was bone. I did not know it was multiple. I hold that too now. His wrist in my head, my five years, the residue — and all the other things that broke open tonight.

I do not know what that means. I do not know how she knows. I only know my face feels wrong when I touch it and my hands look like someone else's hands and the residue is still drying on my skin. I hold the number in my head the way I was holding the staff. I do not know what to do with it.

I look down at Riley. Still breathing. Still warm. Still here.

A figure steps from the shadows near the cop cars — a man, I think, a shape carrying something in the way he stands. He was not there a moment ago. Now he is. A light opens. Not headlights. Not spell-light. A door — a different kind of door, one that is not supposed to be there. Magic-flare, then gone. He moves to Lady Light. Lifts her. Not rough. Not rushed. The way you carry someone you are not going to drop. He steps through the door. Lady Darkness follows. The door closes. The light is gone.

Just the red-and-blue of the cop lights on the dark road again.

Marisol is beside me.

I do not hear her approach. She is just here — kneeling in the space next to my shoulder, close enough that I can smell something clean and sharp under the blood and the night air. Her hands move toward me. Steady. Practical. Checking.

"Echo. Let me see you. You're fading."

I know she is right. I can feel it. My heart is beating too slow. My body is pulling inward — the way it does when there is nothing left and the engine is still running on fumes. The cruiser door is the only thing keeping me upright.

"Riley—"

Her name comes out of me before I can stop it. Barely a sound. Just breath and scrape. My throat is not my throat and the word tears through it anyway.

"She's stable. Weave has her. Breathe."

Marisol's voice is calm. Not soft. Not hard. Just... true. Her hands keep checking — my pulse, my pupils, the side of my neck where the damage lives. She does not flinch at my face. Does not pause at my hands. Just keeps working. Steady. Grounding.

I breathe. Not because I want to. Because she told me to. Because she is here and her hands are warm and practical and the world feels less like it is sliding sideways when she is in it.

Voices carry across the road — Hicks, I think. Short. Decisive. I catch fragments between the engine hum and the blood in my ears.

"Washington Regional." Pause. "Two hours."

Her voice in the back of my head. __Acknowledged. Cruiser interface ready. Route confirmed.__

"You two, with me." Hicks again — not to me. To Luna and Leo. Luna answers something I cannot hear. Leo's voice threads through — thin, spent, but still there.

Marisol near me. "I'll meet you there."

I put the pieces together in the slow way my brain works now. We are going somewhere. Someone is driving. I am staying with Riley. That is all I need to know.

"Weave. Help me get them in the cruiser. Echo first — she's about to drop."

Marisol's voice. Calm. Direct.

Her voice. __Field adjusting. Lifting Echo.__

The shimmer around us changes. I feel the field reconfigure — particles shifting, redistributing, reading the weight of us the way she always does before she moves. Riley lifts first.

She rises out of my lap. Slow. Supported. The field cradling her head and her shoulders and the curve of her back — the same careful surface of iridescent gold-green shimmer. I feel her weight leave my thighs. My chest goes cold where she was pressed against me. But she is not gone. Not pulled away. The field keeps her close — suspended, still wrapped, still breathing.

Then I am lifting.

The particles shift underneath me and I am rising — my spine leaving the warm metal of the door, my legs untucking from the asphalt. The field holds me at every point. Shoulders, back, hips, the places where my body is screaming. It does not hurt more. Just different. Just moving.

Riley is still right there. Inches away. The field moves us together — two shapes in the same shimmer, repositioning as one decision. I never lose her. My arm stays out. My hand finds her shoulder. The field lets me.

"...Riley."

Not a word. Just the shape of her name. The scrape of it. My throat gives me nothing else.

"I'll see you there." Marisol's voice again. Steady. "She's in good hands."

The cruiser door opens — her doing, or Marisol's, or both. The field guides us inside. The back seat is dark and cool and smells like someone else's life. Riley settles first — head propped, body arranged across the seat with the field still supporting. Then me. I fold into the space beside her. The field adjusts one more time — soft, final — then recedes from the seat but stays close. Still there. Still wrapped around us in a thinner shimmer.

Something warm on my shoulders. I did not feel it arrive. My cardigan. The one I left hours ago. Someone pulled it up over me while I was not looking. While I was holding Riley and the whole world was glass and blood and her breathing.

Beside me on the seat — my brass staff. Snapped clean below the crystal, the two halves resting together. The break looks too neat. Wrong. She went back for it. Went back for both of them. Past the blood and the broken glass and the body on the road.

Weave I try to say her name. My throat will not make the sound. But the shimmer on my shoulders brightens — one quick iridescent gold-green pulse — and I know she heard me.

My hand finds her arm. Her skin is warm. The wrong-light is there — on her arms, visible when the headlight comes through the window. Stopped at her shoulders. Not spreading. Just sitting there, quiet. Like it is waiting. I do not know what it is waiting for. I keep my hand on it anyway.

The cruiser waits.

Engine running. Not moving yet. The road still outside the window. Red-and-blue lights washing across the dark asphalt, cop cars still lined near the trees, the place where the box was. The place where he is.

The caster's body. Shoulders and chest and the neck that is not there anymore. The wrong-light still holding in the wound — a slash of color that should not exist, suspended in the space where his throat was. I ran past it. I stepped around the head. I did not look at his face. I do not look at it now. I just know it is there. Behind us. On the road with the cops and the flashing lights and the cold night air.

Not our problem anymore.

Luna and Leo are walking toward Hicks' cruiser. I see them through the window — two figures on the shoulder, one with an arm tucked, one with an arm cradled against his chest. Leo's halo flashes. Brief gold pulse above his head — visible even from here, a quick flash as his arm shifts and the pain demands it. Then it steadies. He keeps walking. Luna keeps walking beside him. They reach the cruiser. The door opens. They get inside.

The cruiser ahead moves first.

Then we move.

Not fast. Not loud. Just — moving. The engine note changes and the road slides under the tires. Behind us, the Malibu's headlights come on. Amber hazards blink once. Twice. Then it falls in after us.

The road opens in front of us. Dark. Quiet. Headlights sweeping a cone of light across the asphalt. I feel her driving — the car settling into a steady hum, the wheel adjusting in small increments, the engine finding its rhythm. I do not need to see the empty driver's seat. I know who is driving. I know she has us.

{{image: /lore/real-moments/image2-sub-umbris-ad-lucem-vehimur.png}}

I look at Riley.

She is still unconscious. Still breathing. Her face is turned toward me on the seat — cheek resting against the upholstery, the mark from his fist dark on her temple. Her hair is tangled and loose and the blood is matted through the darker strands. Her sweater is ruined. The holes where the blade went through are dark with dried blood. But she is breathing. Shallow. Steady. The rise and fall of her ribs under the stained fabric.

Her hand is near mine. I close my fingers around hers — or I try. My fingers do not close all the way. The knuckles are too sharp. The skin is too tight. These are still not my hands. But her hand is warm. And she is still here.

The road is dark ahead. The headlights sweep the asphalt. We are driving toward something — a hospital, I think, a place where they will look at all of us.

My eyes are heavy. Heavier than heavy. The cruiser hums under me and the seat is warm and Riley is beside me and her hand is in mine — or mine is in hers, I cannot tell anymore which grip is holding and which is being held. It does not matter. Either way she is here.

The wrong-light is still on her arms. I can see it when the headlights sweep through. Pale. Bleeding at the edges. But it is not spreading. It is just there. Like it is waiting.

And I am waiting too. For the hospital. For the morning. For whatever comes next.

But my eyes will not stay open.

The road hums. The stars slide past the window. Riley breathes — shallow, steady, the only sound in the dark. My head tilts against the seat. My hand stays on hers.

I am still here. We are still here. We are still here.

Then I am not.

I come back because something says my name.

Not out loud. In the back of my head. Weave's voice — close, steady, the way she gets when she is trying not to scare me.

__Washington Regional ETA: under two hours. Route clear.__

She keeps talking, I think. Or maybe I dream it. The words come in pieces and I cannot hold the order.

It takes me a second to remember where my body is. Back seat. Cruiser. Dark window. Riley beside me.

Good.

My cardigan is still around my shoulders. The wool scratches softly at my neck. The two broken halves of my staff are still on the seat near my hip. Riley is stretched across the seat with her face turned toward me, breathing shallow and steady. My hand has slid from her fingers to her arm at some point. I leave it there. The wrong-light on her arms is still visible when the headlights catch it. Pale. Stopped at her shoulders. Not spreading.

__Riley is stable. Wound remains sealed.__

My throat works once. "...good."

Barely sound. Enough.

The empty driver's seat is lit gold-green at the edges. The wheel shifts in tiny precise corrections. The dashboard seam glows. The rearview mirror catches the shimmer and gives it back. Fine. Weave has us.

__Marisol is waiting.__

That lands somewhere solid in me. I do not spend the air to answer again. I just keep my hand on Riley's arm and let the road keep happening under us.

I drift.

Not all the way out. Just in and out enough that the road becomes pieces. Headlights sliding over guardrail. The hum under the tires. The soft pressure of the field every time the cruiser takes a turn. Riley's breathing beside me. Once I open my eyes and see the stars moving across the top edge of the window. Once I wake because the cruiser hits a seam in the road and my throat remembers the hand that was there and hates me about it.

She doesn't wake. Good.

I keep touching her anyway.

The dark changes gradually. Fewer trees right against the road. More house lights. Then more than that. Signs. A gas station halo in the distance. Traffic lights hanging over an intersection like something from a normal night.

My brain does not trust normal right now.

__Approaching destination.__

The cruiser takes an exit. Slows. Streetlights smear across the window. Storefronts. Parking lots. A red light. A left turn. I do not know the town well enough to name anything except the feeling of being too lit up after too much dark.

I look at Riley again. Still breathing. Still warm under my hand. Her bag is still with her, the strap curled across her like she never let it go even unconscious. Of course she didn't.
