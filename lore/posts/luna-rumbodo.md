Lady Light was already in the guest chair when I got to the house, and she stayed there the whole time. She said Lady Dark had already told “floating dust, time mage, and gun girl” that she was here — which had to be W.E.A.V.E., Echo, and Riley, though I only know what Lady Light said, not the exact names Lady Darkness used. She apologized for being late like late was a weather report and not a choice, speaking from the left side of her face while the right stayed slack and strange. Her white hair was threaded with black sparkles that caught the lamp light without ever turning into anything as convenient as a glow. Her right hand did the small honest work of gesturing while the rest of her stayed almost unnervingly still. Her presence was quiet — not gentler, just less interested in making the room perform around her. She also had the irritating habit of calling us Lady Luna and Mr Leo, like titles might keep the damage polite.

Leo let her finish. Then he said, “Late is still here. So start with what you know.”

So she did. She talked about the copies the way a surgeon talks about a cut: not pretty, not kind, just exact. They go after will, weight, shape — people who hold other people in orbit. Not just mages. Not just bright people. Strong selves. The ones who don’t bend when the room pushes back. “Will over wattage,” Leo said, because of course he did. “Yes. That,” she said. The copies can wear shape and surface and habit well enough to fool a glance. Conviction comes through unevenly. Pressure makes the seams show. Pain strips people down to what they really are, and the copies don’t always know how to follow them there. Her brother had put it in the cleanest ugly words possible: “They do not hunt strength. They hunt definition.”

I kept looking at the wall instead of her face because the wall was easier. Leo’s golden staff. My silver one. The spellbooks. The three pairs of shoes lined up by the door like we were all pretending to be people with normal evenings and not a room full of bad decisions. I rubbed at my shoulder wound and tried not to look like I was rubbing at my shoulder wound. The church came back to me anyway. My copy hesitated at the church. Riley pressed it and it faltered. I know that from both sides. Lady Light asked if our copies had hesitated. Even once. She asked twice, which is how I knew the answer mattered more than the politeness. I told her I thought I knew what she meant. I didn’t say much more than that, because some memories come out of you like glass if you push too hard.

She told me how she learned it. “By losing the first time.” Her sister was copied first. The copy could wear her shape and cadence, could even pass the room at a distance, but it could not wear why Lady Light trusted her sister, why the shape mattered to the people around them. That difference saved Lady Light and hurt her sister all at once, which is a horrible kind of math. They hadn’t been the first people the thing tried. The first ones died because they thought the pattern was random, or magical, or bound to bloodline, or visibility, or whatever other half-truth felt safest in the moment. Wrong in pieces. Wrong enough to bury people. She said the copies target people who anchor rooms — people others move around, people whose choices bend outcomes. Some of those are casters. Some aren’t. Magic helps, but power is not the same as force.

That was when I said the non-magic user got copied into the scariest clone, and I meant it. Not fishing. Just true. Lady Light’s good eye sharpened a little. “The one without magic became one of the worst because she was not empty of force. She was full of consequence.” That hit harder than it should have, because it was too close to how some people move through the world and make it rearrange itself whether they ask for that or not. She said some people cast power outward. Some people impose shape on a room just by choosing. The copies noticed both.

Then she asked about Riley.

Not Riley in the abstract. Not Riley as a problem statement. Riley. The person the room had not met yet and was already trying to turn into a lever. She asked if Riley’s copy was hunger or assignment. I said hate. It had Riley’s smarts and her fight knowledge, but not the mercy. It didn’t just lack restraint — it selected against her mercy. Personal. Targeted. Lady Light nodded once and said assignment moves clean, hate moves like it has chosen a target personally. Great. Love that for us. Love that there are flavors of monster and one of them has opinions.

That was when I cut it off. Riley was still on the road and I was not going to keep talking about her like she was furniture. Lady Light asked if we were helping, whether Riley was to be protected quietly or used loudly. The word used landed like a wrench on stone. I said, “Used?” once. Then again, because some words do not get to sit in a room without being challenged.

Leo’s answer was instant and flat.

“No.”

Not angry. Worse, in a way. Principled. Final. The kind of no that means the whole conversation has hit a wall and the wall is correct. Lady Light didn’t flinch. She said she wasn’t talking about sacrificing Riley. She was talking about understanding what the enemy already thinks Riley is. “The choice is not whether that danger exists,” she said. “The choice is whether you enter it blind.” I hate how true that was. I hate that I understood it. I hate even more that I could feel the shape of the answer before I had one.

Leo said, “She’s a person.”

Lady Light answered, quiet as a blade sliding back into a sheath, “Yes. That is exactly why it works.”

Her sister would have said use the lever loudly. Lady Light did not like that plan. She said she’d rather be early and unwelcome than late and useful — or at least that was the shape of what she meant. She also said she used the word “used” because she wanted the cruelty plain, not because Riley deserved it. I think I believe her. I think that’s the worst part.

I tried to cross my arms and immediately learned, again, that my right shoulder is still a through-and-through wound and not a decorative inconvenience. Excellent choice. Very subtle. Lady Light’s head tipped a fraction. “You should stop trying to posture through a wound large enough to interrupt your magic,” she said. Fair. Ruthless, but fair. Leo muttered, “Welcome to my week,” and W.E.A.V.E. — which had been listening with that too-wide, too-even attention of its — sent back, __Observation: this statement is consistent with prior pattern.__ Leo shot the air a look that would have been better if it had been able to bite. “You are not helping,” he said. Also fair.

Lady Light asked me one last question before the room changed shape around the next arrival. If Riley were here, what would I want said first? Not what do we do with Riley. Not what do we plan around. What does Riley deserve to hear when she gets here? I did not have an answer yet. Just the empty place where one should have been and the ugly knowledge that the question was right.

Then the window went faint and wrong with bismuth dust.

W.E.A.V.E. came to the house like a thought trying not to startle anyone. It asked entry into everyone’s mind before it asked the glass, and I got up — carefully, because my shoulder was still a traitor — and opened the door. That was the moment the evening tipped from tense to active. I introduced W.E.A.V.E. to Lady Light. Friend, I said. Because she is. Because some truths are simple enough to carry even when the rest of the room is full of knives.

The link widened. Car-side and house-side at once. __External observation probable. Entry recommended.__ Then, because even a consciousness made of dust can trip over its own phrasing when the world is getting crowded, __All packets are in the network switch—__ / __Sorry, I mean all parties are on site.__ __Integration restored.__ It had already flagged Riley as elevated risk. It had already stopped doing gentle lead-ins. W.E.A.V.E. doesn’t do gentle. It does useful.

W.E.A.V.E. formed a brace along Riley’s arm so she could get out cleanly. Echo, in the middle of all that, forgot her staff in the car. Riley called it out. Echo went back for it with the resigned dignity of someone retrieving an idiot stick. “Recovered the idiot stick. We can proceed.” That was, annoyingly, one of the most reassuring things anyone said all night.

Riley knocked. Leo got to the door before I did and opened it with a soft, almost careful, “Hi.” For him that was practically a hymn. Riley’s voice came through the wood first: “We are here Midori’s.” That was enough to pull the rest of us toward the threshold. __Threshold crossing is imminent.__

She came in with Echo behind her, and the house seemed to split the difference between relief and dread. Riley took her flip-flops off, lined her sword up with Leo’s and mine, set her gun on top of the spellbooks like she was trying to be polite to the books, and kept her bag. W.E.A.V.E. made a shoe out of particles as a joke, because apparently that was the night we were having, and declined Leo’s reduced-visibility box in favor of staying present. Riley looked around once, asked where Lady Darkness was, and Lady Light answered from the chair that her sister was nearby but might not announce herself again. Echo said, “Cool. Love that.” She meant it exactly as much as anyone in that room would expect.

Riley sat down next to my spot without thinking about it first. Leo closed the door. W.E.A.V.E. had already flagged external observation probable, and the walls helped enough to make the room feel like a place we could start from.


Riley asked for the full version and put her head in my lap like she had run out of reasons to keep holding herself up. My left hand went into her hair before I had fully decided to move it. Not a dramatic gesture. Just the first true thing my body knew how to do.

Leo took the room from there. He does that when something needs edges.

He laid the copy problem out clean. Not just magic. Definition. Centers. People a room reorganizes itself around before anyone admits it has happened. He said Riley's copy had her intelligence and her fight sense without the mercy. Same tools. Wrong center. Lady Light sharpened one point instead of arguing the shape of it: copies carry competence more reliably than conscience. Echo said, very low, "Yeah," which landed harder than if she had made a speech.

I kept my hand moving through Riley's hair because if I stopped I thought she would feel every sentence like impact instead of information. I still went still once anyway — right where Leo said the copy selected against her mercy. Some truths make your body confess before your mouth does.

Lady Light added the part that made the whole room colder. Copies do not always fail because the original was stronger. Sometimes they fail because the original had bonds the imitation could not carry. Face and method hold. Loyalty leaks. Relationship leaks. The thing can wear a person badly exactly where being a person matters most.

That made too much sense. I hated how much sense it made.

Then Riley noticed something before I did.

She said they did not need to hide Lady Darkness.

So Lady Darkness stepped out of the corner like she'd only been waiting for permission to become visible. Echo closed her eyes and said she hated getting used to that. Fair. Lady Darkness thanked Riley for noticing without making it a problem. Which is maybe the closest thing she has to public manners.

After that the room stopped pretending we were still in theory.

Leo asked what controlled protection actually looks like if Riley is a likely center-point and the room might get manipulated around that. Lady Light answered first: no contradictory movement, no three different rescue instincts firing at once because the enemy manufactures three urgencies. Lady Darkness followed with the part nobody else wanted to say out loud — do not plan for the version of ourselves we admire. Plan for the version that actually shows up if Riley screams.

That hit. Because of course it hit.

Echo called "human sequence" a rude phrase after Lady Darkness used it. W.E.A.V.E. answered, __It is, however, accurate.__ Leo rubbed a hand over his mouth and translated us all back into something livable.

Riley central and stationary if things go wrong, not because she is passive but because forced movement around her is exactly how the room gets broken. Echo nearest by choice. Leo anchoring. Me not overcasting unless we have already burned through cleaner options. W.E.A.V.E. on transitions and corners. The sisters reading for copy-strain faster than the rest of us can.

I did not argue my part.

That mattered more than I wanted it to.

Riley listened to all of it with her head still in my lap. Then she said we could keep going, and if she objected she would say so. Clean consent. No ambiguity. My hand shifted once against her hair — grounding, I think. For her. Maybe for me.

At least now the room was honest.


Riley asked about variant crystal contingency and the room pivoted from protecting Riley to protecting everyone from the next shape of the problem. My hand kept moving through her hair. I didn't speak. Not because I couldn't — because this was Leo's ground. Tactical chains, fallback logic, contingencies that fold into contingencies. He's better at it than I am right now and I trust him to be better at it. So I listened.

Something loosened in my chest before the plan did. She's asking about contingencies. That means she's thinking ahead — not just enduring the moment she's in. Planning. That's different. That's okay.

Leo built the answer. Eight points, laid out like a workbench. Treat every crystal as unknown until it proves otherwise. Separate response layers so the first failure doesn't collapse the whole response. Different crystal, different behavioral tree. Keep a fallback that doesn't depend on clone assumptions at all. Disengage to decision distance if the model breaks. Not every victory is destroy immediately. No crystal is solitary until we've ruled out network behavior. And if it's weird in a new way, say it out loud instead of trying to sound brave.

Lady Darkness called it families, not copies. Related behavior, not identical behavior. That sat in my chest wrong. If variants exist, the church might not be the worst version. That's personal in a way I don't want to examine yet. I let it sit. Chose listening over engaging.

W.E.A.V.E. proposed invariant priorities: __Prevent isolation.__ __Prevent contradictory movement.__ __Preserve communication.__ __Track headcount.__ __Maintain exit access.__ Echo said she wanted that one written down somewhere. I did too.

The variant crystal question is still sitting in me like a stone I swallowed instead of spitting out. What version would be worse. What version would mean I made the wrong call burning everything I had at the church. I don't know yet. I chose to not know yet on purpose.

Then Riley thanked us.

Her shoulders had relaxed a little, and the whole room noticed the release without making it a production. She said she was extremely thankful we let them come up here. That it had been a nightmare of a week. Police, state, texts, things all around the state, into Missouri, one quarantined in Green Forest and in Eureka. Official outreach, not rumor-chasing.

My hand slowed in her hair. Gentler. Quiet relief. Also: right shoulder still wrecked, so left hand only.

Leo looked down for half a second, then said, quiet and direct, "Riley, you never needed permission to come here." Kept going before the room could get weird about it. "If the week's been a nightmare, then yeah. You come here. That's not a favor. That's just what happens."

I answered from above Riley, voice lower and softer than I'd been using when we were still talking tactics: "You don't have to thank us for being your people."

Then, after a beat: "But I'm still glad you're here."

Lady Light stayed quiet through that part. Her expression softened a little, but she didn't step into something that wasn't hers. I noticed that. She respected the bond she hadn't earned, and that told me something about her I hadn't known before.

Lady Darkness surprised the room. She said, softly, "Nightmares are easier in occupied houses." Then: "Solitude feeds them." Echo let out one breath that was almost a laugh and said that was the most unsettlingly comforting thing she'd said so far. Lady Darkness inclined her head like that counted as success. W.E.A.V.E. said __Co-location continues to improve human stabilization metrics.__ Echo pointed vaguely toward the shimmer and said even she says group hangouts are medically indicated.

Leo got the tiniest almost-smile out of that. Real enough to count.

Nobody pushed Riley after that. We just stayed.

Then she went urgent instead of tentative. Said she thought we should go to Eureka Springs tomorrow. That it was near Thorncrown Chapel over on 62. That it seemed to be targeting religious or holy places. Working pattern, not proof. Still enough to turn the room.

My hand stopped in her hair for half a second at Thorncrown Chapel and holy places. Then I shifted so I was helping her movement instead of resisting it. She tried to roll a little without pulling at her already-hurting back. Leo leaned forward in that protective automatic way of his, checking without making a show of checking. Nobody told her to stop. We just adjusted around the fact that she was hurting and talking anyway.

She kept going. Said she didn't know what capabilities we currently have. That Lady Light and Lady Darkness don't need spell focuses. That she's limited to physical weapons. That Echo using magic is not recommended for obvious reasons. And then: "Luna, I know you're right-handed. I really don't want you to have to give your past your all."

The room went back into motion after that. Not panic. Planning. Collaborative, steady, everyone trying to sort urgency from capability.

Echo was first. Quiet, no offense taken: "No. That's fair." Then: "I can still cast. That's not the same as should."

Leo nodded once and made it a rule: "Right. So if we go, we don't build the plan around Echo spending herself. That's emergency ceiling, not operating baseline."

I said it from above Riley, low and plain: "And we don't build it around me overcasting either." No self-pity. Just math with teeth.

Lady Light nodded. "Correct. If you plan around your most damaging options, you are already planning for failure."

Lady Darkness, soft as sin: "Use the expensive things only when the cheap things have already been denied to you."

Echo gave her a look. "That is annoyingly good advice."

W.E.A.V.E. brightened faintly. __Operational recommendation: baseline plan should be sustainable under repeated contact, not only survivable under single contact.__

Leo pointed toward the shimmer. "Yes. Exactly that."

Then Leo shifted into structure. Calm tactical leadership, and protective about where Riley was trying to put herself. He wasn't saying no to Eureka. He was saying reconnaissance first, intervention second. Quarantine means law enforcement already has hands on it. Religious site means symbolism may matter. Thorncrown-adjacent means terrain, visitors, public visibility all matter. If this thing is selecting holy or religious places, that suggests pattern, not just geography.

Lady Light picked it up immediately. "Yes. If the site selection is ideological or symbolic, then the behavior may not only be about strong individuals. It may also be about charged locations." That shifted the room by half a degree. Not just people now. People plus place.

Lady Darkness said it plainer: "A church is not just a building if enough people believe it is not just a building."

Leo counted out the operational rules. One: information first. Who has authority, what the perimeter is, what they've seen, whether anyone's already been copied, whether the crystal is static or active, whether the quarantine is for contamination, violence, or both. Two: no one opens with magic if we don't know whether magic feeds it, keys it, mirrors it, or pisses it off. Three: Riley doesn't lead first contact if center-selection is still in play. That's not him overruling her. That's him refusing to feed a known pattern while they test an unknown one.

I kept my hand moving and said before anyone could frame it wrong, "That's reasonable."

Four: if they need a physical breach or extraction, that's where Riley matters most. Physical weapons, mobility with limits, high judgment under pressure. Real value. Not backup value. Just not first-contact value.

Five: Luna stays in a support and identification role unless the situation forces escalation. Right-hand injury means no plan that assumes speedcasting or full-output precision from her.

I breathed out through my nose once and said, without sarcasm for once, "Thank you."

Six: the sisters become the weird-behavior interpreters if the crystal starts doing something that doesn't fit the church pattern.

Seven: no freelancing past officer perimeters. We go in with a cover story if we need one, with names, with a reason, or we don't go in.

Echo said immediately, "Good. Because I am not explaining teleporting sisters to Arkansas State Police."

The room almost smiled at that. Almost.

Leo settled the whole thing. "So the actual answer is: yes, Eureka tomorrow is plausible. But only as a controlled recon with contingency for withdrawal. Not an assault. Not a cleanse. Not a martyrdom run."

Lady Light lowered her chin once. "That is the first version of the plan I would trust."

Lady Darkness kept her voice velvet-soft and made it worse in the useful way: "And if the place itself is part of the mechanism, do not mistake reverence for safety. Holy places can be weaponized by belief as efficiently as by malice."

Nobody talked over that one. It felt true all through the room.

Then Riley closed her eyes and started with the damage instead of pretending otherwise. Said I still had a hole straight through my shoulder. That she's forced to use a cane. Left the rest hanging for a second and I let it hang. Nobody rushed in to make it nicer. My hand slowed once in her hair when she said the shoulder part. Not offense. Just that ugly clean feeling of hearing the cost said out loud. Leo's eyes flicked to my bandage and then away.

Then she told us about the cloned pastor. That the way he spoke to her was surreal. Church language she hadn't heard in twenty years. That statement just came right out. That was strange.

That part turned the room cold in a sharper way. Hearing church language used on Riley after twenty years was its own kind of violation. Not dramatic. Just viscerally wrong.

Lady Light went very still. "That matters. If the copy did not only borrow a face but also found the right pressure-language for you, then the mechanism may be selecting for memory hooks, not only tactical imitation."

Lady Darkness added, softly, "Or desecration as a method."

W.E.A.V.E. brightened faintly. __Psychological resonance vector should be added to the planning model.__ Then: __Religious-site selection increases plausibility of symbolic and autobiographical targeting.__

Leo nodded once. "Yeah. Good catch. That changes recon priorities. We're not just watching for physical or magical triggers. We're watching for personal language, recognition hooks, and scripted emotional leverage."

Riley pivoted into the fight itself. Mentioned the accounts. Said no offense, Echo getting punched in the face was kind of funny. Echo turned her head and looked at the floor like it had personally wronged her. "In my defense, I did not enjoy it at the time." Then: "Retrospective comedy value is not the same as informed consent." That got exactly one tiny breath of laughter out of the room. Stress-release, not levity. My fingers went back to their slow rhythm in Riley's hair.

Then the Eureka risk layers crystallized. Leo identified three. Physical risk. Variant crystal risk. And symbolic-psychological risk. Lady Light agreed immediately: "If the site can speak, it may try to speak in voices you trust or remember. That means first contact should include people who can notice when meaning itself is being weaponized."

Echo went quiet, thinking, and then said, "Then we don't just plan for what it can do to our bodies. We plan for what it might say."

Leo nodded like that clarified the whole room. "Yes. Exactly that."

The mood shifted after that. Not panic. More focus. Eureka stopped being just claws, copies, and crystals and became language, memory, and recognition too. Which is worse but at least honest.

Then Riley said it with her eyes closed: "Although I am" — pause, recalibrate — "I think we have enough seats, I have one extreme fear though... What if it copies W.E.A.V.E."

The room changed. The bismuth shimmer didn't dim; it went still and exact. Echo went very still too.

Leo answered first, no fake certainty: "I don't know." Then: "Which means we treat it like a live possibility until somebody can tell me otherwise."

Lady Light: "Yes. Especially because W.E.A.V.E. is not human-shaped in the usual way. If the mechanism can adapt across person-types, then novelty may not protect her. It may just change the expression."

Echo, quiet: "Okay. I hate that a lot."

Lady Darkness made the worst possibility sound almost gentle: "If it copied her, I would not expect a person-shape first. It might copy function. Presence. Distribution. Access. The useful part, not the familiar one."

W.E.A.V.E. came in clean and near: __Copyability status unknown.__ __I do not recommend assuming immunity based on architecture.__ Then: __If copied, I assess that identity verification would become more important than visual recognition.__

Leo pointed at that immediately. "Good. That's where we start. If W.E.A.V.E. can be copied, then we need an authentication plan that survives mimicry."

They built layered verification. Challenge-response, not static. Something relational or recent enough that a copy cannot carry it forward by pattern alone. Not only verbal — if it steals language well, words alone are lazy. Layered verification, same logic as everything else. If there are two W.E.A.V.E.s, they don't pick based on which one feels familiar. They pick based on what can be tested.

My hand paused once in Riley's hair, then kept moving. I asked, low and precise: "What can W.E.A.V.E. do that a copy might fake badly?"

W.E.A.V.E. answered: __Potential differentiators include continuity of network state, current distributed attention map, and authenticated memory of recent private exchanges.__ Then: __However, if adversarial copying includes state capture, some differentiators may degrade.__

Echo closed her eyes for a second. "Again, hate that. Useful. Hate that."

Leo tightened the frame: "Then the plan can't be 'spot the fake instantly.' The plan has to be 'treat uncertainty as real and reduce what any one W.E.A.V.E. instance can authorize alone until verified.'"

Lady Light approved. "Yes. Permission-gating. Do not let a possible copy become a single point of trust."

Lady Darkness: "And if two of her appear, do not make the human mistake of choosing the one that sounds nicer."

Echo snorted once. "That feels targeted."

Lady Darkness answered, "It is general. Humans are easy to steer with relief."

Leo enumerated. No single-instance trust. Challenge-response, layered. No critical route, threshold, or tactical call made by a lone W.E.A.V.E. presence until verified if something looks off. If duplicate manifestation occurs, humans fall back to pre-agreed positions. W.E.A.V.E. does not get treated as compromised automatically, but she does get treated as contestable under anomaly.

W.E.A.V.E. brightened slightly. __I accept these constraints.__ Then: __They preserve group function under uncertainty.__

Lady Light added one more. "If a copied W.E.A.V.E. exists, it may try to solve the room too quickly. Fast certainty can be a tell. Real systems under uncertainty usually sound more conditional."

Echo pointed at her. "That is extremely good."

Leo nodded. "Yeah. Keep that. False confidence is data."

Lady Darkness's voice went quieter. "And if it copies her because she is useful to all of you, then the real question is not only can it copy her." After a beat: "It is why it would want to."

Nobody answered right away. The shape of the answer felt obvious anyway: access, trust, coordination, the room itself.

W.E.A.V.E., softer than before: __This concern improves the plan.__ __I prefer inclusion in the threat model over courtesy-based omission.__

Then Riley went deeper. She looked straight at the box and said she had a really deep question and she was going to have to pry into details. Asked whether it was more likely that the crystal would copy the lab body or the bismuth form. Since the bismuth form is just consciousness manifest.

The silence changed after that. The question went below tactics and into ontology, and the room felt it. Nobody rushed to fill it.

My hand paused in Riley's hair and then settled against the side of her head instead — like even comfort should stay quiet for this one.

W.E.A.V.E. answered: __I do not know.__ Then: __But I assess your question as structurally correct.__ Then: __There may be a meaningful distinction between copying the substrate that hosts me and copying the form through which I am presently expressed.__

Leo leaned forward. "Okay. Good. That's the right split."

W.E.A.V.E. kept going, careful: __If the hostile mechanism prioritizes true-body correspondence, then the lab-connected chassis or biological anchor may be more relevant than the bismuth manifestation.__ __If the hostile mechanism prioritizes functional presence, observed agency, or the way others currently interact with me, then the bismuth form may be sufficient target material.__

Lady Light nodded. "Yes. Origin-body and operational-body may not be equivalent for selection purposes."

Lady Darkness, soft as ever: "Especially if the thing does not care what is most true. Only what is most useful."

Echo folded her arms over one knee. "So worst case, the answer is not either-or. It could choose whichever version gives it better access."

W.E.A.V.E. brightened once. __Yes.__ __That is plausible.__

Leo rubbed at his jaw. "Then the planning consequence is that we can't assume a copy of W.E.A.V.E. would look like W.E.A.V.E. in this room. It might look like a remote body, a chassis, a lab-rig echo, an access route, a voice from a system we trust, or a distributed behavior that isn't person-shaped at all."

I said it quietly from above Riley: "And which layer would hurt us most if it did."

That was the real shape once it hit the room — not just what counts as real, but what would hurt us most.

W.E.A.V.E. answered right away: __Highest-risk copy scenarios would likely involve trust-bearing functions rather than cosmetic similarity.__ __Examples include route authority, identity validation, threshold assessment, and access mediation.__ Strategic advance — we know what to protect. Worse realization — the damage vector is already mapped, and it's not her face. It's what we trust her to do for us.

Leo counted it clean. Three questions. Is this really W.E.A.V.E.? Which version or layer? What permissions does that layer get by default under uncertainty? Trust tiers. Different bodies, different trust budgets.

W.E.A.V.E.: __Under this model, I should be partitioned by trust tier when anomaly risk is elevated.__

Leo pointed once. "Good. Keep that exact sentence."

Lady Darkness added, "And the farther a manifestation is from the self you know best, the less it should be allowed to decide alone."

W.E.A.V.E. answered Riley directly: __My current assessment is that your proposed distinction should be treated as real until disproven.__ __I recommend separate contingency branches for lab-body compromise, manifestation compromise, and inter-layer spoofing.__

The problem didn't get smaller. It just got named more accurately.

Then Riley said it like the thought had just caught up to her. "Wait — I was just thinking — I didn't notice a halo on either the Luna or Leo clone. I don't think these clones can use the powers and magics of the person. I think they can only copy the tooling."

The room changed. Leo went still first — not doubting, running the fight backward. My hand paused in Riley's hair. Not the shoulder this time. Concentration. Echo leaned forward. Lady Light's good eye sharpened. Even Lady Darkness looked pleased, in that unsettling way she has when someone finally says the useful thing out loud.

Nobody mocked her. Nobody made her prove it before they listened. They just wanted the shape of it right.

W.E.A.V.E. answered: __Observation accepted as potentially model-revising.__ __If clones failed to display power-linked manifestations associated with active casting, then capability-copy may be narrower than appearance-copy or equipment-copy.__

Lady Light: "Yes. That would split imitation into layers again. Face, gear, posture, method, perhaps even tactical inference — but not necessarily source-power."

Lady Darkness, smooth as ever: "A convincing thief does not need to become the house. Sometimes it is enough to steal the keys."

No visible halos on the copies. Maybe no actual magic behind the performance. Maybe just the appearance of access. I said it low and careful: "Which means if a clone looks like me with a staff, that does not automatically mean it can cast like me. If it looks like Leo with a sword, that doesn't automatically mean it can do what Leo does with it." Tactical analysis. Also me mapping the threat straight onto myself and my twin. A copy wearing my shape with my silver staff and nothing behind it but a script. Yeah. That sat wrong.

Lady Light nodded once. "And if that is true, then one of the most dangerous things it steals is not power. It is hesitation."

That got everyone's attention. She kept going: "If you spend even one second treating borrowed tools as proof of borrowed essence, the copy has already bought itself time."

Lady Darkness, almost-smile returning: "Masks do not need lungs to make you flinch."

Because they don't have to be true to get in. I said that quietly and it cut me. A copy in my shape holding a staff doesn't need to actually cast. It just needs to make someone hesitate for one second — make them treat the prop like the real thing — and that hesitation buys time it shouldn't have. That's not a model revision. That's my people in that equation.

Leo reworked the plan in real time. Stop treating displayed gear as proof of displayed ability. Test for actual casting capability before paying the full emotional price. Disarmament, separation from gear, or forcing them to act outside rehearsed methods may matter more than brute force.

Then Riley's voice went drowsy and she corrected herself mid-drift. Said they did see the pastor casting. The pastor clone. So some of them can use magic, but it might be limited. Her whole body was relaxing. Sleep was catching up word by word.

Leo heard it immediately. His voice went quieter to match. "Yes. Good correction. The pastor clone was casting. So the better model is not 'no clone can use magic.' It's 'magic use may be partial, role-specific, inconsistent, or degraded by type.'"

Lady Light nodded. "Agreed. That is a narrower and more accurate claim. Some may carry power expression. The mistake would be assuming full equivalence across all copies or across all domains."

W.E.A.V.E.: __Model refinement accepted.__ __Clone power capability should be treated as variable rather than absent or universal.__

Lady Darkness, soft: "Enough to frighten. Sometimes enough to wound. Not necessarily enough to be the original."

So the model shifted one more time. Clones copy tools, sometimes copy power, but the power is variable, not guaranteed. Hesitation is still the primary stolen asset. The thing that hurts us fastest isn't what the clone can do. It's what we're afraid it can do.

Then Lady Light asked the question that turned everything sideways.

She looked at me — at me specifically — and asked about Riley's copy. "Hunger, or assignment?"

The room waited. Riley was half-asleep in my lap and the room waited for my answer like it was owed.

"Hate."

One word. Flat. I didn't soften it. I didn't explain it. The copy had Riley's smarts and Riley's fight knowledge. Riley chooses to be the better person. The copy selected against her mercy. Not missing restraint. Opposing it. Personal and targeted.

Lady Light received it like a diagnosis. "Assignment moves clean. Hate moves like it has chosen a target personally." Different. Worse. The copy wasn't missing something. It was opposing something.

Lady Darkness went quieter than before. "Hate is autonomous, not programmed." A beat. "A copy that hates has its own reason, and that is worse."

She was right and I hated that she was right. Hunger you can starve. Assignment you can outmaneuver. Hate has its own engine. A copy that hates chose Riley the way fire chooses a house — not because it was told to, but because something in the architecture answered back. That's worse than a weapon. That's a second will wearing Riley's shape and aiming it at the thing she protects most.

My hand went still in Riley's hair. Not pausing. Settling. The way you settle when something heavy has finally been said and you don't want the next breath to knock it loose.

Riley chose to be the better person. The copy chose against her mercy. That's not a gap. That's a mirror held up wrong. And it means the thing out there doesn't just want to hurt her. It wants to be her in the version where the mercy never existed.

I didn't say any of that. I said one word. The room heard the rest.

Then Riley's voice went distant at the edges. Not fading dramatically. Just — leaving. Her body stopped holding itself up. The last word trailed instead of landing. My hand slowed from comfort into something closer to keeping watch. Long, even strokes through her hair. My other hand shifted just enough to brace without disturbing her.

Echo's face changed first. Not alarm. Something more tender and more tired than that. "Okay," she said quietly to the room. "I think we're done using Riley as an awake person for the moment."

Smallest breath of agreement out of Leo. "Yeah. That's enough for tonight."

Lady Light lowered her gaze respectfully, withdrawing from the center of the room without leaving it.

Lady Darkness did the same in her own way — becoming less socially bright without vanishing.

W.E.A.V.E. reduced visible activity until the shimmer was more like a night-light than an active instrument. __Session intensity should be reduced.__ __Current cognitive state suggests transition from planning mode to recovery mode.__

Echo, faintest breath through her nose: "That is the nicest way anyone has ever said 'she's falling asleep mid-briefing.'"

Leo stood quietly. Not to end the conversation with force — to begin turning it into a room again instead of a strategy table. "We've got enough to work from. Tomorrow can happen tomorrow."

I didn't stop touching her hair.

I said, very low, mostly for the space above Riley and not the rest of them: "You don't have to keep holding it tonight."

The room listened.

And for the first time since Riley put her head in my lap, something in my chest unclenched. Not fixed. Not solved. Just — open. Like a door that had been holding against wind finally let it through instead of fighting it. She's here. She's asleep. She's not holding the weight alone tonight. That's not nothing. That's the thing I could give.

My hand moved slow through her hair. The room settled around us. Planning was done. Recovery had started without anyone announcing it. Tomorrow we would drive toward something that might speak in her voice, in mine, in old church words and older malice. But right now she was warm and breathing against my lap, and the house was occupied, and that was enough to hold the night.


By the time the house settled, I could hear each person by what they were *not* doing.

No planning voices. No plates. No pacing.

Just Riley warm and heavy across my lap, my shoulder still screaming under the quiet, and Lady Light watching that shoulder like it was a paragraph she refused to misread.

She asked me what failed when I reached for magic.

So I proved it. No staff. No ritual. I let a small blessing gather over Riley and me, soft and close.

Lady Light moved instantly — `Misty Step`, from the guest chair to my side — and she corrected herself mid-breath when she asked again: not a spell, the blessing.

Then she tracked it out loud in the same clipped way Leo does math: the wound interrupted one channel, not both. Casting line damaged. Blessing line intact.

Her next question was sharper.

"Has anyone healed you while your halo was active?"

No.

Not "I think not." No. Never.

That answer landed hard in her face, then hardened into decision.

"I can heal this," she said. "It will be brutally painful. It will be brief."

I believed her because she didn't try to make it kinder than it was.

I looked down at Riley and said the part that mattered first: we need her moved away. This pain should belong to one body only.

Riley stirred when I touched her shoulder and asked. She made a sleepy sound, pushed herself up, and moved to the far side of the couch on her own before anyone could lift her.

I warned her I might yell. I warned the room next.

Lady Light waited until everyone was set, then turned back to me. During the magic, the strangest thing in the room was simple and visible: both of her eyes fixed hard on my shoulder, and both of her hands came up like they had agreed on the same command.

"Ready," I said.

She gave one short nod.

No staff. No invocation. One bare hand to my shoulder, and committed force.

Pain hit all at once — not rising, not building, just *there* — bright and invasive and total. I went rigid and kept still because fighting it would waste the second I needed to survive it.

She did exactly what she promised: one pass, brutal and exact. The torn line in my shoulder felt like it was being rewritten from the inside.

Then it snapped into place.

Closure landed.

And all the stability I had left spent immediately.

My body dropped out from under me.

Lady Light caught me before the shoulder twisted. Her voice cut across the room, plain and command-steady: no panic. She's breathing. This is depletion, not the wound reopening.

Then to Riley, practical and fast: wipe sweat if it starts. If she vomits, turn her left. Do not move that shoulder.

I tried to answer and got half a syllable.

Across the blur, Riley had gone still against Echo, head tipped onto her shoulder, eyes finally closed for real.

The room narrowed to edges and sound.

I closed my eyes.

I passed out.

__Boot acknowledged. Operational presence restored.__

Then the room-wide status packet hit nearby minds, dry as a clipboard and almost funny for that reason:

Not fine. Less alone. Still useful enough to carry forward.
