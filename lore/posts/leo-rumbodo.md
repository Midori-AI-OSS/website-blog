She was already sitting when I came back into the room.

Lady Light. White hair with black sparkles. Only the left side of her face moved. The right side didn't. Right hand doing all the work.

She apologized for being late.

I didn't interrupt. Said: "Late is still here. So start with what you know."

She explained what they'd learned about the copies. They're not drawn to everyone the same way. They target people with shape in them -- will, weight, people who hold others in orbit. Not just magic. Strong selves. Strong direction. People who don't bend easily.

"Will over wattage," I said.

She agreed.

They learned because the copies failed in patterns. Shape, surface, habits -- those copy well. Conviction, they understand unevenly. Pain strips people down to what they really are. The copies don't always follow them there.

Her brother's phrase: "They don't hunt strength. They hunt definition." Same thing. Different words.

She asked if our copies hesitated. Even once.

I already saw that. The Luna-copy hesitated when Riley pressed it back in the church. Called it pattern leakage then. Still holds.

Luna noticed the swords, the staffs, the spellbooks. Three sets of shoes. Rubbed the wound on her right shoulder -- the through-and-through she can't move that arm through and keeps pushing on anyway. Asked Lady Light how she learned all this.

"We learned badly," Lady Light said. "By losing the first time."

I went still at that. Losing landed hard. Not protective. Just processing.

She kept going. She and her sister weren't the first target. Just later ones who stayed alive long enough to compare notes. The ones before them didn't survive. They thought the copies were random, or based only on magic, or bloodline, or visibility. They were wrong in pieces.

Copies target people who anchor rooms. People others move around. People whose choices bend outcomes. Some were casters. Some weren't.

"Magic helps. But power is not the same as force."

She asked again if the copies hesitated.

I asked how she learned this. Lady Darkness was copied first -- their first encounter, the one they lost. Overwhelmed. That's how they started comparing. First-hand data from the worst possible source.

I asked about baiting copies wrong. If you could misdirect them by presenting the wrong kind of target. Theoretical. She treated it as theoretical too, but didn't dismiss it.

Then she suggested using Riley as a lever against her copy.

Using her.

I said no.

Flat. Immediate. No negotiation in my voice because there's nothing to negotiate. You don't use people. That's not just me being protective of Riley -- that's principle. Riley is a person, not a tool. You don't turn people into bait.

Lady Light's word was "used." She chose it deliberately. Clinical, to make the cruelty plain. I don't think she was advocating for it. I think she was laying out the option so we'd reject it out loud.

The conversation kept drifting after that -- toward what to do about Riley, what to decide for her, how to position around her. And Luna stopped it. Said we don't have this conversation without Riley in the room.

Lady Light asked her last question then. Not what do we do with Riley. What does Riley deserve to hear when she gets here.

Luna didn't have an answer yet.

Her halo flickered. That's her unconscious pain dampening -- she doesn't always notice when the wound hits harder. I moved a step closer. Instinct. Through-and-through right shoulder and she keeps pushing anyway.

She got up for the door at some point. I told her to sit.

"She's right about the timing," I said. "Not about deciding for Riley." Timing matters. But Riley decides for Riley.

"Welcome to my week," I muttered after. Luna's been pushing through injuries all week. This is just more of the same.

Lady Light said "Oh?" when the halo flickered. Something about it caught her attention. I don't know what she saw. She didn't explain.

W.E.A.V.E. noted my pattern: __Observation: this statement is consistent with prior pattern.__ Accurate. Unhelpful.

"You are not helping," I told her.

Then W.E.A.V.E. arrived at the window. __I am W.E.A.V.E.__ / __Thank you for arriving before the inbound pair.__ Luna opened the door for her, introduced her to Lady Light. And the Mind Link kicked in -- both halves now broadcasting to both groups. __Integration restored.__ The link was split before and I didn't know.

Tokens came through the link in sequence: __Integration restored.__ / __External observation probable.__ / __Entry recommended.__ / __Threshold is clear.__ / __Interior tension elevated.__

__External observation probable.__ Someone's watching. Or something. W.E.A.V.E. flagged it. That's enough to treat it as real.

Threshold clear -- no one standing in the way of the door.

Interior tension elevated -- yeah. Lady Light still in the guest chair. Luna's wound. The conversation we just had about using Riley. Tension's the right word.

Through the link, Echo called Riley "extremely competent, mildly judgmental." That's Echo's rhythm. Even relayed, the tone was clear.

W.E.A.V.E. kept updating through the link: __Door-state remains stable.__ / __Interior parties are aware of external arrival.__ / __Threshold crossing is imminent.__ She was tracking the approach.

Then the glitch. W.E.A.V.E.'s signal stuttered -- that old modem sound, the garble. Not normal. Filed it. Don't know what caused it.

Lady Light didn't move. Still sitting. Still watching.

Luna might have moved toward the door. I was tracking her and W.E.A.V.E. at the same time.

Then Riley tapped on the door. "We are here Midori's."

That's Riley. Short. Direct. She's on the other side of the door and I can't see her.

The car group is here. They made it.

I don't know what happened in the car. W.E.A.V.E.'s link gave me arrival status, not context. No idea what Riley and Echo talked about. No idea what they saw on the way here.

Got to the door first. Not pushing past Luna -- this is just what I do when the people I love are on the other side of a barrier. One hand near the knob, my staff visible deeper inside where I'd left it within reach. Controlled enough to pass for calm if you don't know me.

"Hi."

That was enough. Relief and -- yeah. Contained. Didn't need to say more.

Echo said "Hi" back. A little breathless from the short walk and the whole night.

Eyes went to Riley first. Then the cane. Then the bismuth brace on her arm. Then Echo coming up the walk with her staff. Taking inventory. That's what it is. Making sure everyone who walked in is accounted for and intact.

Exhaled once. "Come in."

Whatever comes next, at least the threshold is over.

W.E.A.V.E. brightened. Through the link: __All parties are now co-located.__ / __Conversation-state may resume.__

Riley came in slowly. Flip flops off at the door. W.E.A.V.E. shaped a shoe out of particles for a second. Riley and Echo both noticed. I let it go. Sword up with mine and Luna's and the gun on their spellbooks. Everyone knowing exactly what every weapon in the room means. Bag still on her.

I closed the door behind them.

The watched feeling -- the one W.E.A.V.E. flagged as __External observation probable.__ -- dulled once the door was shut. Didn't disappear. Just... less. Walls help.

"You're welcome," I said. Quiet.

Riley looked around. "Thank you, looks like... we are missing one?" Then: "Lady Darkness? I think."

Lady Light answered first: "My sister is nearby. That does not always mean visible."

Echo, coming in behind, muttered: "Cool. Love that."

Lady Light added: "Lady Dark said she would meet you here. She did not promise to announce herself again."

"That seems like a flaw in the plan," Echo said.

Almost smiled for real this time. Almost.

Echo lifted her staff once, deadpan: "Recovered the idiot stick. We can proceed."

That got the tiniest visible shift out of me. Almost a smile, or the memory of one.

W.E.A.V.E. chose: __Reduced visibility container remains optional.__ / __Current preference is active presence.__ Small exhale. Good. Right call. Keeping her in a box when the house is being watched doesn't make sense. Active is better than contained.

Riley sat down next to Luna's spot without thinking about it. Some things don't need deciding.

"We were talking before you got here," I said. "But Luna stopped it where it needed stopping."

Short version first. Nobody gets blindsided if I can help it.

I said the copy issue is not just magic. It is definition. Centers. People other people move around without meaning to. That means Riley, yes. Not because we treat her like bait. Because pretending the enemy would miss what the room does around her would be stupid.

I kept it plain after that. Lady Darkness is nearby. That is not the same as gone. W.E.A.V.E. confirmed the threshold matters and the risk-state changed when Riley came inside. And Luna stopped us before the conversation turned into decisions about Riley without Riley.

Lady Light took the next part herself. Good. It was hers to own.

"I used a cruel word," she said. "I do not retract the danger. I do retract the distance. You are not a tactic. You are a person the tactic may try to form around."

Riley asked for the full version. Then she lowered her head into Luna's lap like that was the first safe place she'd reached all night. Luna's hand settled in her hair on instinct.

"Okay," I said. "Full version, then. Lady Light, correct me if I get any of this wrong."

I kept it structured.

Lady Light put one line on it clean: copies carry competence more reliably than conscience. We'd already seen that much in the church. Riley's copy had her fight sense with the restraint carved out. That was the part I wanted said plainly.

Then the rest, clean from the top. Riley is not just at risk because of magic. She is a pressure point because rooms move around her. That makes her useful to the enemy in two directions at once: as a target and as a way to predict formation. Lady Darkness wants to force the strike early. Lady Light wants layered protection and controlled thresholds. We were using Lady Light's version.

Lady Light added the last piece herself. Copies do not only fail tactically. They fail relationally. They can wear the face and keep the method and still miss the part that makes loyalty hold. That means hesitation matters.

Riley lifted her head just enough to say, "You don't need to hide Lady Darkness."

Luna's hand paused once in Riley's hair. Both of them were already looking toward the same corner.

The dark in that corner shifted into a person.

Lady Darkness let the room settle before she spoke. "I was not hiding. I was simply not insisting." Then to Riley: "Thank you."

Fine. Variable accounted for.

So I moved us to the part that matters more than theory.

"Practical version," I said. "If Riley is the pressure point, what does protection actually look like?"

Lady Light answered first. No contradictory movement. No improvising different rescue vectors. No splitting attention in three directions because the enemy can make three things look urgent at once.

Lady Darkness made it uglier and therefore more useful. Plan for actual reactions, not aspirational ones. If Riley screams, who moves. If Echo gets hurt, who moves. If Luna casts, who covers. If I anchor, who watches the edges. If W.E.A.V.E. splits attention, who keeps the sequence coherent.

Echo said, "Human sequence is a rude phrase."

__It is, however, accurate.__

"Fine," I said. "Then the assignments are these. Riley central and stationary if things go bad. Echo nearest by choice. I anchor. Luna doesn't overcast unless cleaner options fail first. W.E.A.V.E. watches corners and transitions. The sisters call strain faster than the rest of us."

That was the working version. Not an order handed down. A shape we could use.

I looked at Riley. "Anything in that you want changed before we treat it as real?"

She answered clearly enough for the whole room. "You all can continue. I feel as though you're making a good tactical decision. If I object I will let you know."

"Good," I said. "If anything starts sounding wrong to you, you stop it."

She agreed. That was explicit enough for me.

Morning came in thin and gray.

I was at the stove before anyone else was fully awake, keeping the noise low and the house steady. Riley came up off Echo's shoulder slow, then whispered toward me, asking where Echo's staff was.

"Against the counter," I told her. "I moved it before waking Echo last night and never handed it back. Reflex plus panic plus hardwood is a bad first five seconds."

I kept my voice down and gave her the rest clean. Luna was in the guest room, flat per Lady Light's orders after the collapse. Both sisters were still with her.

Riley asked if I'd spar with her before we left for Eureka.

"Yeah," I said. "Sword work, not gun. Scheduling only. After food, after I check Luna, and only if the room still says go."

She nodded and started down the hall with the cane anyway. Not rushing. Still urgent in that way she gets when care and fear are holding the same space.

I checked the doorway first.

"Status?" I asked.

Lady Light answered without looking away from Luna. "Good. She hasn't done anything stupid yet."

Protective sarcasm, not mockery. I took it as a green light with limits still attached.

Riley reached Luna a second later. The check-in was short and loaded -- one look at Luna's face, one look at the shoulder line, and all the words she didn't use sitting there anyway. Luna wanted table instead of bed. Lady Light made her take it in steps.

By the time we were settling plates, the house had shifted from overnight watch to functional morning.

Then Riley stood to ask for soda, caught the chair wrong, and went face-first.

I was beside her before the chair finished tipping. Lady Light checked with me, precise and level. Pupils. Wrists. Answer first, then stand.

Riley got up slow and said the floor had critted her dignity.

"Dignity injury confirmed," I said, handing the cane back. "No fresh disaster."

Echo, still half asleep from the couch, managed: "You fell out of breakfast. Rude."

Two short beats, enough to reset breath without pretending it was funny in any deeper way.

Riley spotted the box, tossed a napkin at it, and called for bootup.

Bismuth leaked through the seam, then lifted into a clean hover over the table. The token hit both channel and room where nearby minds could hear it.

__Magic twin (Luna) is seated. Right arm active. Area currently stable.__

That was followed by a drier addendum, almost a joke and still operational: __Riley has fallen once.__ __No new hostiles detected.__

Breakfast finally happened.

Luna tested in careful increments. Right-hand grip on the mug held. Fork control held. Then she reached wider and the shoulder line caught hard at the edge -- not tearing, not collapse, just a physical refusal at casting angle that made her jaw lock.

I brought her staff over and set it within reach so we could stop guessing.

Grip worked.

The second she tried to cant it into a real casting lane, the shoulder blocked again -- abrupt stop, visible strain, frustration immediate and sharp on her face because her hand was back enough to tempt the move while the joint still wouldn't give it.

She set the staff down like she wanted to throw it and chose not to.

Lady Light tracked the motion and called the split: wound closure held, function still constrained.

Lady Darkness gave it to us in one line. "closed is not restored."

Nobody argued.

I reopened Eureka planning before the table could drift.

"Recon first," I said. "Intervention second. That stays the principle."

W.E.A.V.E. confirmed in tokens over the center of the table: __Reconnaissance-first framing remains consistent.__

Then role passes, clean and explicit. Echo remains emergency ceiling, not baseline. Luna no overcasting. W.E.A.V.E. maps perimeter and exits. The sisters read strain and false-center behavior faster than we do.

And for Riley, I said it exactly so nobody could distort it later: "Riley sequenced not sidelined."

She looked at me once, then nodded.

Echo started loading the Bolt like a retreat plan instead of a hero story -- water, chargers, med kit, food that survives delay. Good logistics, no speech about courage.

I confirmed spar timing before departure: sword-only, light contact, cane included, no proving.

Near the end of breakfast, Echo said she didn't want today built out of fear or built out of the lie that together meant fine.

Luna answered low, tired, and exact.

"We're still damaged. We're not carrying it alone, and we can work from there."

I let that sit a second, then gave the only answer I had.

"Less alone is enough to build with."

The spar ended without ceremony.

Yard quiet. Breathing rough. Blades lowered.

Riley stepped back first, brought the sword in, and said we should move while this still counted as a plan. I nodded, wiped my grip once, and seated glimmersteel into the scabbard. No flourish. Just done.

We shifted straight into load-out. Luna moved beside me with her staff and sword, careful with the shoulder and still exact about sequence. Echo took Riley's Bolt. Riley rode passenger. Luna and I took the Malibu. W.E.A.V.E. stayed spread across both vehicles.

Before engines, Riley held up one hand and made the quarantine call from the driveway. I heard her keep it clean and in order.

Names first.

Plates second.

Intent third: reconnaissance support, perimeter coordination, no freelancing inside officer lanes.

W.E.A.V.E. description last: one distributed bismuth-swarm intelligence, one mind spanning both cars.

The response on the other end stayed neutral and procedural -- route confirmation, acknowledgment, hold for perimeter command relay, continue inbound with no independent entry.

When she hung up, Luna asked if they sounded calm.

Riley adjusted her bag strap, checked her cane tip placement on the concrete, and answered without looking up. "No. Organized. Better."

We rolled.

The morning should have felt ordinary and didn't. Too still in the trees, too little motion in the shoulders. I kept both hands steady on the wheel and watched the mirrors while Luna watched the ridgeline.

Across the shared link, W.E.A.V.E. sent the first flag:

__Charged-location concern remains compatible with prior threat model.__

I acknowledged with a short nod she could read through the link. No argument. We were already treating Thorncrown like a live site.

The next step hit from the Bolt side first.

__Brief luminosity event observed.__ __Cause unresolved.__

I did not get direct eyes on the flash from the Malibu. Reported, not witnessed.

Then Riley came over hands-free with the stronger warning.

"Leo, regroup before final approach. Subway lot on the edge of Eureka. The air feels wrong."

Echo's voice cut in right after, clipped and flat. "Copy. Regroup now."

We turned in.

The lot looked ordinary in the way bad places do right before they stop pretending. Old stucco, tired trim, empty spaces, too much daylight for the way my spine felt. Nobody went inside.

Riley gave us the sequence from the Bolt: sky pulse with no weather source, then the window crack, then the outside air reading off-pattern. Not immediately hostile. Not clean either. Like the site had already been occupied by something that wasn't done with it.

W.E.A.V.E. confirmed cross-vehicle agreement:

__Deviation confirmed across parties.__ __Risk flag: non-ordinary site state.__

Luna's grip tightened on her staff, then eased by force. "Thorncrown was never going to let us walk up like a clean errand," she said.

Then red and blue washed the lot hard.

Officer Hicks came in fast, brakes short, one hand already on radio while the cruiser was still settling.

"I've got them," he said into the mic. "Subway lot, west side. Church party is here."

He looked at us once and moved straight to urgency. Combat was active at Thorncrown now, not hypothetical, not contained theory. Active.

Riley asked who was involved.

Officer Hicks gave it cautious and tight: reports indicated two very powerful casters on site had been cloned -- one light-presenting, one dark-presenting. Same pair, duplicated. Officers already in place were trying to hold spread at roadside and were losing ground.

Riley gave him our approach in under twenty seconds: called ahead, recon-first, no independent perimeter breach, stop made for anomaly verification before entry.

Echo compressed it exactly where it needed compressing. "Site got weirder before we got eyes on it."

Officer Hicks accepted that, then gave terms: stay in motion, stay in lane, follow lead lights, do not improvise outside officer geometry.

W.E.A.V.E. threaded through the link again:

__Reconnaissance-first logic remains valid.__ __Tempo increase required.__

Riley requested consent for W.E.A.V.E. to place a partial thread in the cruiser for shared continuity. Officer Hicks gave it.

A narrow stream of gold-green bismuth lifted from our side and slipped through the cruiser's open window.

__Consent received.__ __Lead-vehicle thread established.__

Roster and convoy held to departure lock. Those profiles stayed report-only on our side of the line.

Formation locked exactly: Officer Hicks cruiser first, my Malibu second, Riley's Bolt third.

Officer Hicks's lead lights flared.

We pulled out in locked order and kept it there.

Officer Hicks in the cruiser up front. Me with Luna in the Malibu behind him. Echo driving Riley in the Bolt behind us. W.E.A.V.E. running through all three at once, one mind stretched thin on purpose.

Hicks had already said combat at Thorncrown was active. That stayed in the car with us louder than any music.

I kept both hands on the wheel and set distance by brake-light rhythm, not comfort. Luna rode angled toward her good side, jaw tight, staff braced where it would not torque the shoulder if I had to stop hard. In the mirror I could see Riley in the Bolt shifting around the cane and bag, making room for movement before movement was needed.

The road folded through the last Ozark hills in short rises and blind curves. Guardrails flashed and dropped away. Driveways vanished into trees and did not come back out.

We were not driving blind. We were driving on two confirmed cues we had already logged before convoy roll:

- a clean wrong pulse in the sky with no weather source
- outside air that felt wrong -- not hostile, but off, like a place already in
use

That second one stayed with me the most. I could still feel it in the back of my throat with the windows up.

__Cross-convoy link remains stable.__

__Lead-light tracking nominal.__

The words were calm. The bandwidth wasn't. You could feel the strain in how little extra there was.

Hicks's lightbar kept painting the road in red-blue bands that made every tree line look like it was stepping closer between flashes. Radio noise leaked from his cruiser when we hit straight sections. No full sentences. Enough to hear urgency and cutoff calls from units already on scene.

Luna watched the shoulder, then the tree breaks, then the road back to center. "Still approach-only," she said, low.

"Still approach-only," I said back. "We don't touch first contact unless contact finds us."

In the mirror, Echo kept the Bolt centered and close enough for one-move support, far enough for one bad stop. Riley had her head turned toward Hicks's lights like she was counting the flashes.

__Threat environment remains active ahead.__

__No downgrade indicators detected.__

No one asked for reassurance. No one offered any.

I held the Malibu in lane under Hicks's lead lights and kept us moving toward the fight we had not reached yet.
