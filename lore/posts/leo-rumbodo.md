She was already sitting when I came back into the room.

Lady Light. White hair with black sparkles. Only the left side of her face moved. The right side didn't. Right hand doing all the work.

She apologized for being late.

I didn't interrupt. Said: "Late is still here. So start with what you know."

She explained what they'd learned about the copies. They're not drawn to everyone the same way. They target people with shape in them — will, weight, people who hold others in orbit. Not just magic. Strong selves. Strong direction. People who don't bend easily.

"Will over wattage," I said.

She agreed.

They learned because the copies failed in patterns. Shape, surface, habits — those copy well. Conviction, they understand unevenly. Pain strips people down to what they really are. The copies don't always follow them there.

Her brother's phrase: "They don't hunt strength. They hunt definition." Same thing. Different words.

She asked if our copies hesitated. Even once.

I already saw that. The Luna-copy hesitated when Riley pressed it back in the church. Called it pattern leakage then. Still holds.

Luna noticed the swords, the staffs, the spellbooks. Three sets of shoes. Rubbed the wound on her right shoulder — the through-and-through she can't move that arm through and keeps pushing on anyway. Asked Lady Light how she learned all this.

"We learned badly," Lady Light said. "By losing the first time."

I went still at that. Losing landed hard. Not protective. Just processing.

She kept going. She and her sister weren't the first target. Just later ones who stayed alive long enough to compare notes. The ones before them didn't survive. They thought the copies were random, or based only on magic, or bloodline, or visibility. They were wrong in pieces.

Copies target people who anchor rooms. People others move around. People whose choices bend outcomes. Some were casters. Some weren't.

"Magic helps. But power is not the same as force."

She asked again if the copies hesitated.

I asked how she learned this. Lady Darkness was copied first — their first encounter, the one they lost. Overwhelmed. That's how they started comparing. First-hand data from the worst possible source.

I asked about baiting copies wrong. If you could misdirect them by presenting the wrong kind of target. Theoretical. She treated it as theoretical too, but didn't dismiss it.

Then she suggested using Riley as a lever against her copy.

Using her.

Flat. Immediate. No negotiation in my voice because there's nothing to negotiate. You don't use people. That's not just me being protective of Riley — that's principle. Riley is a person, not a tool. You don't turn people into bait.

Lady Light's word was "used." She chose it deliberately. Clinical, to make the cruelty plain. I don't think she was advocating for it. I think she was laying out the option so we'd reject it out loud.

The conversation kept drifting after that — toward what to do about Riley, what to decide for her, how to position around her. And Luna stopped it. Said we don't have this conversation without Riley in the room.

Lady Light asked her last question then. Not what do we do with Riley. What does Riley deserve to hear when she gets here.

Luna didn't have an answer yet.

Her halo flickered. That's her unconscious pain dampening — she doesn't always notice when the wound hits harder. I moved a step closer. Instinct. Through-and-through right shoulder and she keeps pushing anyway.

She got up for the door at some point. I told her to sit.

"She's right about the timing," I said. "Not about deciding for Riley." Timing matters. But Riley decides for Riley.

"Welcome to my week," I muttered after. Luna's been pushing through injuries all week. This is just more of the same.

Lady Light said "Oh?" when the halo flickered. Something about it caught her attention. I don't know what she saw. She didn't explain.

W.E.A.V.E. noted my pattern: __Observation: this statement is consistent with prior pattern.__ Accurate. Unhelpful.

"You are not helping," I told her.

Five: disengage to decision distance. If the situation compresses, pull back far enough to see the whole board. Close quarters favor the enemy's speed, not our coordination. Lady Light: pride kills pattern recognition. Distance often restores it.

Six: variable victory conditions. "Win" might not mean the same thing every time. Define success for the moment, not for the war.

Seven: no solitary assumptions. We move as a unit or we don't move. No crystal gets treated as solitary until we've ruled out network behavior.

Eight: say the weird out loud. If something feels wrong or off, name it. The thing nobody says is the thing that opens the gap. Lady Light nodded at that one. "Especially that."
