The porch light is on. Riley is already at the door — keys in one hand, cane under her arm, sword across her back, gun on her hip, bag over her shoulder. One week out of the ER and she is carrying enough hardware to fight a war on the way to a friend's house.

I don't do pity. I pay attention. There is a difference.

She asks if I want to drive. Says she doesn't feel safe doing it herself. The keys come first, then a juice box. The juice box gets me — just a little. I hold it up once. "Full service." It is easier than saying what I actually mean.

Gold-green dust drifts through the porch light and disappears into the dark. W.E.A.V.E. Bismuth-thin. I track it with one glance. Noted.

I tell her I'm driving. Then I tell her thank you for saying it before trying to prove otherwise. That mattered more than she probably thinks.

I step in close enough to take some of the load without making it a thing. "The cane stays. Sword can come. Gun can come. Bag can come. You do not have to win against the concept of being injured on the way to their house."

"That would be a stupid side quest."

It would be.


The Bolt chirps when I unlock it. Low white lights bloom across the driveway, no engine noise. Just the handle, the weight shift, the night air, Berryville humming somewhere far off. The dash wakes in blue-white across my face when I settle in. I set the juice box in the cupholder like it matters.

It does.

I don't start the car right away. I look at her across the console instead. Ask if she's good getting in, or if she wants me to come around and stop pretending I wasn't already about to.

Riley gets in. Cane first, then the OS bar, hauling herself up. I am already half out of my seat — not because she can't do it, but in case the sword snags or the bag catches. It all clears. Only then do I let myself breathe.

She says she still feels bad about "letting that happen to me." Like it drives her mad. The church. The whole chain of it.

I shut my door. I don't put the car in gear.

I turn toward her. "Hey. No. You did not let that happen to me."

Somebody shot at us. She got us out. She got me inside. She stayed between me and that thing — the purple one — until her body gave out. That is not the same as letting it happen. I hold her eyes so she hears it.

My gaze drops once to the cane, then back up. I know what I'm looking at. I know what it cost.

I tell her: if she starts doing that thing where she makes herself responsible for every insane thing in Berryville, I am going to need the juice box immediately. I mean it. Her guilt spirals are hard on me and I need snacks to stay regulated when the stress climbs.

Then softer. She can be mad. She can be rattled. She can decide churches are cursed forever. Fine. But I'm not taking that one from her. Not as guilt. Not like that.

I reach for the shifter. The Bolt wakes the rest of the way — low electric hum, no engine. "You buckled?"


Riley buckles in. "Yes ma'am."

I snort. "Do not 'yes ma'am' me in my own stolen car." It is hers. She knows that. The joke keeps the air from getting too heavy too fast.

I click in, check her buckle with one glance, shift the Bolt into drive. Porch light slides off the hood. Farmington falls away behind us in dark porches and washed-out streetlamps. Sixty-five miles to Berryville.

I say it again, steady, eyes on the road: "You did not let that happen to me." I am not soft about it this time. I do not need to be. She got us out. She got me inside. She stayed in front of the thing until her body quit before she did. That is not the same as letting it happen. I need her to hear that the same way I need the car to stay on the road — automatic, non-negotiable.

My fingers tighten once on the wheel. Then I say the part that matters: if she starts carrying every insane thing like it only happened because she was not enough, I am going to get mean.

I ask her what she is really worried about. Seeing Luna like that again — or seeing Leo pretending he is holding together better than he is.


Riley holds up the darkness ammo between her fingers, turning it in the dash glow. She says safety is not fully safe. Says there are crystals all over — other people who did not have a Luna, or powers.

I look at the round before I look at her. Not avoiding. Giving the thought somewhere to land first.

I do not argue. My jaw tightens once. "Yeah." A beat. "That is the part I can't argue with."

I ease off the accelerator without meaning to. Crystals everywhere. Copies everywhere. Not every place gets a Leo through a door or a Luna willing to burn herself hollow — sixty-four Photon Blade Swarms when eight to twelve is already a lot, light going cold blue, emptying herself completely. Some places just got caught with ordinary people and bad timing.

That should bother her. It bothers me too. I say so.

But then I look at her — quick, sharp, honest. "You are not allowed to turn that into: therefore everything is on me." That math is poison.


Riley pulls out her phone. The WEAVE app. She types something. A moment later the screen says __roll down the window__. Then she asks — "We have a guest, may I roll down the window for a moment?"

I glance at her phone. Then at the edge of the headlights where the gold-green drift has been trailing us all night. The answer was already halfway here before she asked.

"One, that is deeply ominous out of context." Because it is.

"Two, yes." I hit the window control. W.E.A.V.E. has been with us since the porch. Might as well let her in.

The passenger window drops. Night air pushes in — cool, thin, road-dust and spring damp. For a second nothing. Then the gold-green thread lifts off the dark shoulder and comes apart into finer bismuth shimmer. Catches the passing light. Streams in through the gap instead of away from it.

Not a full body. Just presence. Starting to form something — a silhouette, maybe. The awareness is sharper now that she is inside. The dust gathers along the inside of the door first, then drifts over the dashboard. Slow. Careful. Like something that does not want to startle us.

Some of the particles catch on the juice box in the cupholder. It gleams for half a heartbeat. My juice box. My comfort thing. She touched it with light.

I watch that happen. One eyebrow up. "Okay. Hi."

Then: "You are allowed in the car. You are not allowed to judge my driving." I mean it. She could take the car if she wanted. She could control it. But I am the one driving. That is not a restriction on her. It is just true.


Riley rolls her window up. The road noise drops out. Cabin goes close — just tire-whisper, electric hum, the soft plastic click of her seating a clip. I hear it. I know what that sound is. I don't look. I'm driving. But I heard her load the gun and I know what it means and I am not going to pretend I didn't.

W.E.A.V.E. comes through on Mind Link, careful and close: __I am not fully with you.__ / __I am with you enough.__ / __Enough to observe. Enough to speak. Enough to come further if needed.__ She is split. Part of her is here in the car. Part of her is still elsewhere.

I breathe out through my nose. "Cool. Horrifying. Useful." All three. At the same time.

I say I think safe got damaged. Then I say damaged is not gone. Locked doors still matter. Going together still matters. Telling me she doesn't trust herself to drive — that still matters.

W.E.A.V.E. says __Threat persistence is probable.__ / __Threat totality is not confirmed.__ I side-eye the shimmer. That is somehow less comforting than I wanted.

I ask what she thinks is still with us exactly. The crystals? Or the feeling that nowhere counts as normal now?

Riley says — whatever made those things.

I go quiet. Then: yeah. Not just the crystals. The mind behind them.

W.E.A.V.E. says __The source actor remains uncontained.__ / __That is a reasonable fear.__

I hate when she says something completely fair like that. Because that is the part I keep circling too. Crystals are objects. Copies are events. Those things got made by someone who thought all of that was acceptable. I am connecting dots. It is not confirmed. But if somebody can do that once, on that scale, then yeah — I get why Riley's brain doesn't want to classify safe as real right now.

I say maybe the trick is not asking yourself whether the world is fully safe again. Maybe the question is whether we can still make pockets of it anyway. Even if it's temporary. Even if it's partial. Even if it's just a car on the road, with a gun, a sword, a cane, me, and a dust cloud that keeps using terrifyingly precise vocabulary.

W.E.A.V.E. says __I can refine vocabulary selection if preferred.__

I snort. No. Now I'd miss it.


Riley says she has bad vibes. Really bad vibes. Her gut is telling her something is going to get real, and having us all together would make it easier.

She starts giving me directions — "turn left up here at" — then stops herself. Says "45." She is thinking while she is talking. The bad vibes are real enough that she is pulling apart mid-sentence.

She asks if it would be better to let W.E.A.V.E. drive.

I do not laugh. I do not brush it off. I flick the blinker on and take the left at 45. Then I say: okay. I am taking that seriously.

W.E.A.V.E. comes through on Mind Link: __I can drive if asked.__ / __I have done so before.__ / __I currently assess observation as more useful.__

Yeah. That is where I am at too.

I say she can drive. But if she drives, then she is driving. Right now I would rather have her watching. Observation is the better use of her.

And I would rather Riley have one normal person in the front seat doing a normal thing. That is me. I am the normal one right now. I am driving. That is the most useful thing I can be — steady, predictable, hands on the wheel.

Riley's voice, dry: "You, normal?"

I give her the driest look I have. "Riley. I am incredibly normal. I'm driving an electric car at night while armed, haunted, and being supervised by sentient glitter. This is a standard Tuesday."

W.E.A.V.E. says __I can maintain roadwatch.__ / __I can assume control with minimal notice if conditions change.__

Great. Horrible. Great. All three at the same time again.

I ask Riley: when you say bad vibes, do you mean ambush-bad? Or reunion-bad? Ambush-bad is something on the road. Something waiting. Reunion-bad is Luna and Leo. Seeing them again after what happened at the church. I need to know which kind of wrong we are heading into. One I can swerve around. The other I have to walk into.


Riley is looking at her phone to check her notes when we come up on the stop sign. We need to turn right. She looks up.

She sees something. "Umm... did you see that?"

Then I look up.

My whole posture changes.

Something in the ditch. In the treeline. Side or profile — I couldn't tell. Dark person in the dark. White sparkles in their hair. That is what I saw. That is what Riley saw.

I was doing about sixty. I slowed immediately when she said something.

"Yeah," I say. Already checking the mirror. "Do you want me to keep moving, or do you want me to slow enough to get a better read as we pass?"

W.E.A.V.E. comes through on Mind Link: __I registered a roadside figure.__ / __Humanlike silhouette.__ / __Dark presentation.__ / __Reflective or luminous elements near the hair.__ / __I can maintain visual track if we continue.__ / __I can also mark location if you choose not to engage.__ Same thing we saw. She felt it like a radar bump — anomaly detection, not identification.

She says she can maintain visual track if we continue. She can also mark the location if we choose not to engage. The mark is a mental note. That is all.

I do not know who that was. I do not know if they were moving or standing still. We passed too fast and it was too dark and I am not going to pretend I got more than a shape with sparkles.

"I am voting for not doing anything stupid on a two-hour night drive."

"But I am listening to your gut right now."

That is where it ended. Unresolved. I deferred to her. The car is still moving. The figure is behind us. And I do not know what we just passed.


Something landed in the back seat. A pat sound. No door. No window. Just — there. One second the seat was empty, the next it wasn't.

Riley jumped and pulled the gun. I don't blame her. I nearly put the Bolt into the shoulder doing the same thing in my own way — the car shimmied in its lane and I corrected by instinct.

"Jesus Christ."

Lady Darkness. Sitting in my back seat like she had every right to be there. Dark against the dark. White sparkles in her hair — pinned stars. Same figure from the roadside. Same one. That answers that.

Her hand was on Riley. Light. Grounding, not forceful. She said we were safe. Said "you are the ones from the church shit that happened, yes?" Like she was confirming what she already knew.

W.E.A.V.E. came through on Mind Link: __I did not register conventional entry.__ Neither did I. Because there wasn't one.

I looked at Riley first. "Easy. Don't put a hole in your own back seat."

Then to the back seat: "Okay. Hi. Terrible entrance. Strongly dislike."

I confirmed the church. That was us. Then I told her she was getting exactly one free pass on appearing in moving vehicles like a folklore problem.

I asked her straight: "Why were you on the roadside, and why are you in my car now?"

She did not answer that. Not directly. She asked where we were going. Asked about "the green-haired ones." Luna and Leo. She already knew who they were. She already knew where we were going.

"You knew that before you asked." Because she did.

I asked how she knows them. She did not answer that either. W.E.A.V.E. says __I would also like that answer.__ So would I.

Lady Darkness said her sister is there — at the Midori house — talking to the green-haired ones. Lady Light told her we were coming. She did not share that part herself. I am noting that. She was being strategic. Polite. Not warm.

Then she said she would meet us there. Said she did not wish to make us more uneasy. And she left — same way she came. No door. No sound. Just absence where there was someone a second ago.

W.E.A.V.E. confirmed: __She is no longer in the vehicle.__ I stared at the mirror another second anyway. "Thank you for the update."

Then, more honest: "I hate this state."

Riley put the gun away. The car felt like it exhaled.

Riley said maybe they are trying to help.

I said maybe. She did answer the question — the one about her sister. And she left because she realized appearing in the back seat of a moving car is not a trust-building exercise. That is something. It is not enough.

W.E.A.V.E.: __Her actions are not currently inconsistent with help. That is not the same as verification.__ That is the exact amount of optimism I can tolerate right now.

I asked Riley if she wants to call ahead. "Hey, by the way, a very polite teleporting darkness woman says her sister is already at your house" is not information I want to spring on them from the driveway.


Riley says she had a gut feeling. Feels like they made the right move. Then she stops. Says she doesn't feel ready. Still hurts.

That pause — the one where she checked in with her body before she kept going — I saw it. She decided she could handle it. I am not going to tell her she's wrong about her own body.

She looks up at the road. "Turn left at the gas station." I flip the blinker on and take the left.

I tell her I know. I know she had that gut feeling. And I think she's right — I think we did make the right move.

But those are two different things. Making the right move and feeling ready for the consequences of it. I need her to hear that distinction because she keeps trying to collapse them into one problem.

"You do not have to be less hurt just because your instincts were right."

W.E.A.V.E. comes through. __Pain status remains relevant.__ Clinical. A status call. But also agreement.

I glance at the shimmer. "Thank you, terrifyingly competent glitter." Then, quieter: "She's right, though."

Riley is allowed to hurt. Allowed to not feel ready. None of that cancels out the choice she made. I say it plain.

"When we get there, you do not have to walk in like you're fine."

"You also do not have to walk in like you're the problem."

"You can just be hurt."

W.E.A.V.E.: __That is an acceptable state.__

"Look at that. Group consensus." Three voices in this car and all of us agree: Riley is allowed to exist in pain without apologizing for it.


I offer the call ahead. Do you want me to call, or do you want to just arrive and deal with whatever weird little coven situation is already happening there in person?

Riley says I can't use my staff and drive. She is not wrong. I need my staff to cast. Freehand is not an option.

"Hey Google. Call Luna." The car pings. It rings.

I give her the quickest, flattest look I have. "Wow. Rude. Accurate. But rude." She pivoted away from the emotional territory and I am giving her the out. Playing along. That is how we work.

The ringing fills the cabin. I adjust one hand on the wheel, glance at the display.

"Good," I say. Lower. "Better this way." Better for the Midori house — they get warning. Better for Riley — she gets connection on the way in instead of walking in cold.

W.E.A.V.E.: __I am present.__

"Yeah. That's becoming impossible to forget."

The phone keeps ringing. I say: "If Luna answers, please do not open with 'good news, the darkness lady has a sister.'"

Then, honest: "Or do. Honestly I don't know anymore."

That is not gallows humor. That is me genuinely not knowing how to handle the information we are about to deliver. Lady Light is already at the house. Lady Darkness is on her way. We are driving into something and I do not have a script for it.


The call cuts off. No answer. I glance at the screen, then back to the road. "Cool. Love that."

Riley apologizes. Says she didn't mean to be rude. She is talking about the staff-and-driving comment. The one where she was right but it landed like a crack at my dignity.

"Hey." Softer now. "I know." She wasn't being mean. She was being correct in a way that attacked my dignity. Those are different things. I know the difference. So does she.

Then she says to slow down around the next bend. Remembers sliding her truck around here a few years back. Ice or wet. The kind of memory that lives in the body before it lives in the words.

I take the bend carefully. No drama. No slide. Just tires holding the road. W.E.A.V.E. confirms: __Road condition stable.__

"Good." Then: "And thank you for the warning." Both true.

After the bend straightens: "That one bad?" Then, because apparently I am doing coping humor now: "Because if tonight is secretly just every road in Arkansas deciding to become haunted by personal history, I would like notice."

Riley says "notice given." She is playing along.

Then she says when we get there, she has a feeling we are going to learn a lot. Asks W.E.A.V.E. to start forming at Luna's. Says we REALLY need to be ready.

I give the smallest nod. "Notice received."

The gold-green shimmer along the windshield edge gathers a little brighter, a little tighter. W.E.A.V.E. touches both of us in mindvoice: __Yes. I can begin consolidation near Luna's location. I will leave enough of myself here to maintain contact and observation.__

"Good. Very good, actually." Strategic reconnaissance. We are not walking in blind if we do not have to.

The dust thins in one place, deepens in another. That is not part of her leaving the car. That is her working on controlling the half that is already near Berryville. Nothing leaves us. She is just getting more useful in two places at once.

W.E.A.V.E.: __If your expectation is correct, arrival readiness is preferable to arrival surprise.__

"That is an absolutely terrible sentence that I completely agree with."

I tell Riley I think she's right about learning a lot. Then: "And I think I hate that you're probably right." Being right and being safe feel opposed tonight. That is the part I hate.

"But ready beats not ready." Simple principle. I live by it.

W.E.A.V.E.: __I will notify if I encounter active conditions before you arrive.__

"Perfect. Nothing ominous about that at all."

"Best case, we walk into a weird conversation. Worst case, your gut is ahead of all of us again."

Then the boundary. Firm. Protective. Not just about tonight — about her pattern.

"Either way, when we get there, I'm parking first. Then we decide."

"No rushing out of the car because your instincts light up, okay?"

That is about Riley's general habit of launching herself at problems on instinct. It is also about tonight specifically, because we do not know what is waiting for us and I am not watching her barrel out of this car before we have a plan.


Nobody says much after the roadside. I keep the Bolt pointed toward the Midori house, eyes on the dark and the shoulder and Riley in the seat beside me. W.E.A.V.E. is still in the edges of the air, split and watching. Berryville slides by in washed-out streetlights. The car is quiet in that electric way that feels almost too polite for the night.

Riley warns me about a bend she remembers from sliding her truck there a few years ago. I take it careful. The Bolt holds the road. W.E.A.V.E. says __Road condition stable.__ and I say thank you for the warning.

The Bolt settles in the driveway. Clean stop. No drift, no fumble — just the pedal and the stillness. I leave the cabin light on. The dark out here swallows porch glow and I am not stepping into that blind.

Eyes on the house first. Then on Riley. "Then we do this fast, not messy." That is not bravado. Fast means we do not stand in the driveway spiraling. Not messy means we do not make it harder than it already is.

W.E.A.V.E. comes through on Mind Link: __Integration restored.__ / __External observation probable.__ / __Entry recommended.__ "Good. That I can work with." The first useful thing anyone has sent through the link.

I get out first. Circle around to Riley's side. Offer my free hand — not grabbing, just there. She takes it when she's ready. "I get out first. You take the cane. No heroics between curb and door. If something feels wrong, say it immediately." The rules for the next sixty seconds. Not control. Just how we survive the threshold.

I pick up the cane and put it in her hand instead of assuming she will reach for it. She should not have to ask for the thing she needs. It is already hers. I am just making sure it is where her hand is.

The bismuth settles along Riley's arm — W.E.A.V.E. forming a brace. I glance at it. "And apparently she's got the rest." I do not know what I expected. Something less precise. Something less kind.

"One step at a time." I mean it literally. Driveway. Porch. Door. Not the whole house. Not the whole night. Just the next step.

"Also — if another woman teleports into this driveway, I'm leaving." Half-serious. Fully serious. We have had enough surprise guests for one night.

Riley looks at me. Says she knows about the cost of my magic. Says she cares deeply.

The words land somewhere behind my ribs and I nearly miss a step. Actual physical miss. Foot on the driveway edge, weight shift, correction.

I compartmentalize it. Hard. "I'm going to wait until we're inside before I fall apart about that sentence." That is not a joke. That is a plan. She saw me. She named the cost out loud and said it matters to her. I do not know what to do with that yet. So I am putting it down until I am somewhere I can hold it properly. My thumb finds the edge of one of the belt crystals and stays there. Small pressure. Grounding.

"I know. And I care about you too." That one I say without tripping. It is the truest thing I have said since we parked and it did not cost me anything to say it.

I stay half a pace off Riley's shoulder on the way to the porch. Close enough to catch her. Far enough that she is not being escorted. She is walking. I am just here.

W.E.A.V.E. comes through on Mind Link mid-step: __All packets are in the network switch—__ / __Sorry, I mean all parties are on site.__ Then she catches herself. Resets. Cleaner signal. The glitch passes like a held breath.

It almost makes me laugh. Almost. The first almost-laugh of the entire drive. I let it sit in my chest without letting it out. Not yet.

"You cannot just glitch like that in my head." I mutter it. But it helped. A little. The tightness in my chest loosened for half a second.

"Your house greeting. Or mine?" I give her the choice. She knows these people. She knows what this house means and what walking into it after the church costs her. I am not going to front-run her arrival.

Riley reminds me about my staff.

I forgot it. In the car. I genuinely forgot it. The thing I need to cast. The thing that makes my magic possible. I left it in the Bolt like it was an umbrella... She caught it before I walked up to the house unarmed in every sense that matters.

I froze for half a second. Then, soft: "Wow." A beat. "That would have been humiliating." I point at Riley once, no heat in it. "See? This is why I keep you around. Extremely competent. Mildly judgmental." Then I am already moving — back down the step, back to the Bolt, quick and efficient now that the problem has a name.

W.E.A.V.E. stays with Riley at the threshold while I go back. Through the link: __Door-state remains stable.__ / __Interior parties are aware of external arrival.__ Good. They know we are here.

Riley taps on the door. "We are here Midori's." The sound goes into the house and for one heartbeat everything inside seems to hold still around it. Then movement — not rushed, not panicked. Deliberate. Someone crossing hardwood. The faint shift of a lock.

Through the link: __Threshold crossing is imminent.__

The door opens. Leo is there first — not because he pushed in front of Luna, but because this is what he does when people he loves are on the other side of a barrier. One hand near the knob, golden staff visible deeper inside. His eyes go to Riley first, then the cane, then the bismuth brace, then past her to me coming back up the walk with my staff. He exhales once.

"Hi," he says. For Leo that was almost unbearably soft.

I reach the porch just in time. "Hi." A little breathless from the short walk and the whole night. I lift the staff once, deadpan: "Recovered the idiot stick. We can proceed."

W.E.A.V.E. brightens faintly. Through the link: __All parties are now co-located.__ / __Conversation-state may resume.__ A beat.

Leo glances at the shimmer, then back at us. "Come in." Whatever comes next, at least the threshold is over.

Inside. House rule: shoes off. I kick the flip flops off at the threshold. No ceremony. Just compliance. Riley does the same — flips off, set aside. Then the sword goes up with Leo's and Luna's, three weapons in one place. The gun she sets on the spellbooks. Deliberate. Gun next to magic. A trust gesture or a practical one. Maybe both. The bag stays with her. Not ready to set everything down yet. I understand that.

W.E.A.V.E. shapes her dust into a little shoe next to mine — a dust shoe — then pulls it back into herself. A joke. I see it. I am not giving her the satisfaction of laughing. But I see it.

Leo set out a box on the table — a reduced-visibility container. For W.E.A.V.E. A place she could go if she wanted to be less visible. W.E.A.V.E. considers it for half a heartbeat. Declines: __Reduced visibility container remains optional.__ / __Current preference is active presence.__ Good. I would rather see her than wonder where she went.

The room. I take it in one sweep. Weapons on the walls. Lady Light — present, real. The box W.E.A.V.E. turned down. All of it. I am filing it. The sweep is fast because that is how I process — whole picture first, details after. The weapons register as capability. Lady Light registers as confirmed presence. Everything else is context I do not have yet.

Lady Light. She stays seated. Her left arm barely moves. Something is off about her right eye when she looks our way. I don't have a label for it, just the pattern. I file it away.

That registers. It registers hard. Because I know what it looks like when someone is compensating and not saying it out loud. I know that shape. My hand drifts toward the staff without meaning it to. I let it. I file the observation next to the box and the weapons. Unknown significance. But real.

Riley asks about the dark one. Lady Darkness.

Lady Light answers first. "My sister is nearby. That does not always mean visible."

Lady Darkness is in this room somewhere, in shadow, and I cannot see her. "Cool. Love that." I mean the opposite of what the words say and also exactly what they say. A person made of dark standing somewhere I cannot perceive in the same room as us. Great. Fine. Normal house visit.

"Lady Dark said she would meet you here," Lady Light adds. "She did not promise to announce herself again."

I look at the corner of the room where the dark feels thicker. "That seems like a flaw in the plan."

Leo almost smiles for real this time. Almost.

Riley moves without thinking. Not toward me. Toward Luna's spot. Just gravitation. Instinct. She is next to Luna before she seems to realize she chose it. I do not comment. I tuck a strand of hair behind my ear — a habit I do not bother fighting when I am this exposed — and settle near Riley. Not next to her like I am making a statement. Near her like that is where I go. No performance. No announcement. Just the spot that makes sense.

Leo closes the door behind us.

And the watched feeling — the one that has been pressing against the back of my neck since the porch light in Farmington — halves. It does not vanish. It just... dims. Like the house is holding some of it.
