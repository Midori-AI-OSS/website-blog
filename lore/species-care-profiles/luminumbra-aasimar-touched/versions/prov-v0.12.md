# Luminumbra Care Profile

## Scope

- **Designation:** `Luminumbra Aasimar-touched`.
- **Classification status:** Provisional subtype.
- **Species/profile ID:** `Luminumbra Aasimar-touched / AAS-TCH-LUMINUMBRA-PROV`.
- **Profile version:** `PROV v0.12`.
- **Use:** Shared care reference for Luminumbra Aasimar-touched patient species cards.
- **Primary care flag:** Pain may understate injury severity; halo/eye glow flags pain-reflex power activity; assess structure/function first and use lower-start medication protocol.

## Immediate Triage Flags

- Low reported pain is not proof of low injury severity.
- Body function changes are high-priority escalation signs: weakness, collapse, inability to stand or walk, inability to use a limb, sudden function loss, or similar loss of expected body function.
- Structurally concerning mechanisms still need structural assessment, even when the patient is calmer or functioning better than expected.
- Deformity, neurovascular concern, suspected structural injury, or concerning mechanism should drive imaging and escalation regardless of pain score.
- Visible high pain still matters; treat it as clinically important because usual pain-softening may be failing to keep the patient functional.

## Baseline Anatomy And Vitals

- Mostly human baseline anatomy and vitals.
- Standard monitoring devices are reliable unless a patient-specific EHR says otherwise.
- No known species-level airway or anatomy differences; manage airway as human unless the patient-specific EHR says otherwise.
- Staff should monitor and document active magic signs and magic-state changes alongside ordinary clinical observations.

## Pain Response

- `Lenimen Caelesta` is an involuntary, self-only pain-softening reflex.
- The reflex proportionally softens felt injury pain rather than fully numbing it.
- The reflex cannot be deliberately activated, suppressed, or tuned.
- Pain reports are unreliable as injury-severity proof.
- If injury pain remains visibly high despite this reflex, the underlying injury or depletion may be severe.
- The pain reflex does not heal, stabilize, stop bleeding, restore strength, bypass structural limits, fully numb pain, or ease emotional pain.
- Ask function questions and assess structure/function directly instead of relying on pain score alone.

## Halo And Eye Glow

- Halo and eye glow are known signs of active power or magic use.
- Halo/eye glow during injury pain is a strong indicator that the pain-reflex power is active.
- Track halo/eye changes as clinical context, especially when pain report and injury mechanism do not match.
- Do not treat halo/eye glow by itself as staff danger.

## Medication And Anesthesia

- Medication sensitivity is broad and is not limited to pain or sedation medications.
- Use lower-start dosing and titration where clinically appropriate.
- Normal doses may hit harder, and side effects may be stronger.
- Do not frame this as higher metabolism.
- Analgesia may be less reliable than other drug effects while the pain-reflex power is active.
- Do not keep increasing dose solely to overcome pain-report mismatch; use the EHR/species protocol and direct monitoring.
- No species-level airway difference is documented; use ordinary human airway management unless patient-specific records say otherwise.

## Labs And Imaging

- Labs are mostly human baseline with no special sample-handling issue documented.
- Active magic may create imaging artifacts or alter imaging appearance.
- Image earlier when mechanism, function loss, or magic signs support structural concern.

## Sensory Environment And Communication

- Human baseline sensory environment; no species-level lighting, sound, or proximity accommodation is documented.
- Communication is normal, but pain-score answers should not be used alone.
- Staff should explain assessment steps plainly and include halo/eye changes as clinical context rather than treating them as personality or behavior.

## Handling And Mobility

- Human baseline mobility unless the patient-specific injury, depletion, or recovery state says otherwise.
- The pain reflex does not restore strength or bypass structural injury, so ordinary body limits still apply.
- Do not ignore structural assessment or imaging because the patient is functioning better than expected.

## Inpatient And Discharge

- Standard inpatient observation protocols apply unless patient-specific records require more.
- Discharge instructions should include ordinary return precautions plus weakness, collapse, worsening function, or inability to stand/walk/use the affected body part.

## Do Not Do

- Do not trust low pain as proof of low injury severity.
- Do not dismiss mechanism, deformity, neurovascular concern, weakness, collapse, or function loss because the pain score is low.
- Do not increase medication dosing solely to force the pain score to match the injury concern.
- Do not document the subtype as fully classified.

## Known Unknowns

- Luminumbra Aasimar-touched remains a provisional subtype.
- No additional species-level sensory, monitoring-device, lab-sample, or inpatient-observation differences are documented.
