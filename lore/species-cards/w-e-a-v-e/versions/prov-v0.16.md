### State Transitions

Triage by state, not by human vital signs. Track particle state, communication status, and mode together.

#### Full Mode

- W.E.A.V.E. is visible, active, and able to communicate.
- **Do:** Ask what help is needed, contact any available main-party support, and protect the swarm and environment in parallel.
- **If responsive and movement or containment is needed:** Ask directly, explain the safety reason, and offer simple choices such as box, cup, quiet room, or staying put if safe.
- **If immediate physical loss or damage is seconds away:** Staff may contain first and explain after.

#### Reduced Visibility

- W.E.A.V.E. is in-box, conscious via Mind Link.
- **Treat as conscious, not absent or unresponsive.**
- **Do:** Ask simple status questions and keep the box and particles safe.
- Leave box and particles in place unless there is an immediate safety risk. Contact main-party support.
- Move to a safe room or area away from traffic, fans, drains, and contamination risk if needed.

#### Kernel Panic

- W.E.A.V.E. is conscious but unable to respond, or unstable.
- **Do not declare unconscious or dead just because W.E.A.V.E. cannot answer.**
- Distinguish kernel panic from shutdown using particle activity, NFC / app status, and communication status together.
- **Worsening signs:** Communication loss; particle scatter or loss of cohesion; particles spinning faster, getting brighter, or looking more radiant / holy-like.
- **Do:** Gentle containment, protect particles, contact trusted party or main-party support. No human CPR.
- **Do not:** Vacuum, sweep, discard, or wash away particles. Do not declare unconscious or dead from nonresponse. Do not treat particles as debris.
- **Reboot or bump during kernel panic only if app-directed.** Otherwise contain, protect, and contact support.

#### Shutdown

- W.E.A.V.E. is dormant with no active response.
- Use particle activity plus NFC / app status plus communication status to distinguish shutdown from kernel panic.
- **Bump or boot attempt:** Staff may perform only after NFC or app prompt checks status, sends a boot / wake request, and shows instructions.

### Particle Monitoring

Replaces human vital signs. Monitor:

- **Particle state:** Cohesion, scatter, spin speed, brightness / intensity, and color stability.
- **Communication status:** Mind Link / app / NFC response; whether directed or broadcast communication is stable.
- **Mode / status:** Full mode, reduced visibility, kernel panic, shutdown, or unknown.

Human meds, IVs, blood draws, CPR, pulse ox, ECG, and blood pressure are not useful or applicable to the swarm.

- Staff should answer aloud, by gesture, or by text. Recipients cannot mentally reply by default.
- Use short direct prompts: status, consent, mode, safe contact, support contact.
- Use app or NFC prompts if unsure.
- Staff should use the app-guided protocol for communication; do not improvise.

### Layer Protocol

- Mind Link and Layer testing is okay and recommended when clinically relevant.

- All layers may be available by protocol, but Layer 0 is app-locked: it should not be available unless the app or protocol permits extreme override conditions.

### Containment and Hazards

- **Dry containment:** Bismuth particles can be placed in cardboard or other dry available containers; cups or similar dry improvised containers are fine. Use clean dry paper or card as a scoop or funnel if needed.
- **Magnets:** Not a major concern.
- **Avoid airflow and drains:** Fans, vacuums, vents, drains, sweeping, traffic, disposal, or anything that separates or removes particles.
- **Avoid liquids and chemicals:** Do not wash, disinfect, or chemically expose particles unless specifically cleared.
- **If particles are wet:** W.E.A.V.E. may spin particles extremely fast to dry off; the sound is loud and may surprise nearby staff. Move to a safer space, clear fragile items and people nearby, and use or switch to a dry container or towel-lined area before re-coherence. Contact main-party support.
- **If a particle clump separates from the main swarm:** Treat as sensitive nonhuman material / contamination risk, not trash. Isolate dry and contact support.
- **Particle samples for lab analysis:** Require W.E.A.V.E.'s explicit app or Mind Link consent. Do not take samples without consent.
